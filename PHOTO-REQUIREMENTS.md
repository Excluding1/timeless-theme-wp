## PRIORITY 1: HOMEPAGE (7 photos needed)

### 1. Hero Before Photo
- **File:** `images/homepage/before.jpg`
- **Size:** 800x1000px minimum (5:6 portrait ratio)
- **What to shoot:** Stained, chipped, or yellowed bathtub with dirty grout. The worse it looks, the better the before/after impact.
- **Composition:** Straight-on shot of the full bathtub from slightly above. Include visible grout lines, stains, chips. Natural bathroom lighting.
- **Used on:** Homepage hero before/after slider (left side)

### 2. Hero After Photo
- **File:** `images/homepage/after.jpg`
- **Size:** 800x1000px minimum (5:6 portrait ratio, MUST match before photo angle)
- **What to shoot:** Same bathtub after resurfacing. Gleaming white, clean grout, bright tiles.
- **Composition:** Exact same angle as the before photo. This is critical — the slider overlays them.
- **Used on:** Homepage hero before/after slider (right side)

### 3. Process Photo
- **File:** `images/about/process.jpg` (also used on homepage)
- **Size:** 600x450px minimum (4:3 landscape)
- **What to shoot:** Tradesperson actively working — spraying a bathtub, or applying epoxy grout. Show the person, the equipment, and the bathroom.
- **Composition:** Side angle showing the work in progress. Person should be wearing appropriate PPE (mask, gloves). Professional, not messy.
- **Used on:** Homepage "How It Works" section (desktop only, left column)

### 4-6. Gallery Cards (3 photos)
- **Files:** `images/homepage/gallery-1.jpg`, `gallery-2.jpg`, `gallery-3.jpg`
- **Size:** 600x450px (4:3 landscape)
- **What to shoot:** Three DIFFERENT completed bathroom jobs in different Sydney locations. Each should show a different service (bath resurface, shower regrout, tile resurface).
- **Composition:** Wide shot of the finished bathroom from the doorway. Bright, clean, professional. Include different bathroom styles (modern apartment, older house, heritage bathroom).
- **Used on:** Homepage "Recent Transformations" portfolio cards
- **Text overlay needed:** Location name + service type (added in code, not on photo)

### 7. Service Thumbnails (6 photos — circular crop)
- **Files:** One per service in `images/services/[service-name]/thumb.jpg`
- **Size:** 240x240px minimum (square, will be circular-cropped)
- **What to shoot:** Close-up detail shots of each service result:
  - `shower-regrouting/thumb.jpg` — Clean white epoxy grout lines between tiles
  - `bath-resurfacing/thumb.jpg` — Gleaming smooth bathtub surface
  - `tile-resurfacing/thumb.jpg` — Fresh modern tile finish
  - `vanity-refinishing/thumb.jpg` — Resurfaced vanity benchtop
  - `basin-restoration/thumb.jpg` — Restored basin close-up
  - `shower-sealing/thumb.jpg` — Clean silicone seal in shower corner
- **Composition:** Macro/close-up detail. Focus on texture and finish quality. Bright, sharp focus.
- **Used on:** Homepage service cards (circular thumbnails)

## PHOTO TIPS

### What makes a GREAT before photo:
- Show the worst angle — stains, chips, mould, cracked grout
- Natural lighting (don't use flash — it hides imperfections)
- Include context (whole bathtub, not just one corner)
- Dirty is good. The worse the before, the better the after impact.

### What makes a GREAT after photo:
- Same angle as the before (this is critical for sliders)
- Bright, clean lighting
- Show the whole surface — let the quality speak for itself
- Wipe everything dry — wet surfaces photograph differently
- Remove all towels, products, clutter

### Technical specs:
- Format: .jpg (keep same filename — PHP templates reference by exact path)
- Quality: 80-90% JPEG quality (balances file size vs visual quality)
- Orientation: Landscape (4:3) for service pages, Portrait (5:6) for homepage hero
- File size: Under 500KB per image ideal (will be lazy-loaded)

### What NOT to do:
- Don't add text/watermarks to photos (the website adds labels in code)
- Don't use stock photos (customers can tell — it kills trust)
- Don't mix phone photos with DSLR photos (keep consistent quality)
- Don't crop too tight — leave room for responsive scaling
