## Shipping reality

- **From**: Tullamarine VIC warehouse only (no NSW pickup)
- **Typical shipping cost**: ~$110 per ~3 boxes to commercial address (Sydney)
- **Hazmat surcharge**: +50% for etch/cleaners (class 8 dangerous goods; only one carrier handles)
- **Lead time**: 2-3 days typical, sometimes overnight, occasionally 5 days
- **Bulk strategy**: most operators buy etch/cleaners in bulk every 6 months

## Training requirements (BLOCKER for our resurfacing entry)

Per Bert's emails + transcripts:

1. **Cannot sell acid etch** without training certification
2. **Practical + verbal instruction** required on:
   - Safe use of acid products
   - Use of breathing gear
   - 2-pack spraying technique (at minimum prerequisite)
3. **Two paths**:
   - Travel to NZ (Bert's home) for training
   - Training with one of Bert's "top guys" in Australia (Melbourne)
4. **Bert will accept**: signed statement from Allan + the third party that the sub has been instructed
5. **Cost**: not free — "going to cost some money" but Bert's wording suggests under $2K per trainee

## Bert's strategic advice (quote-unquote, from emails + transcripts)

### On warranties
> *"Anyone putting a 10 year warranty on resurfacing is not in control of themselves. The most you will see is 5 years. Yes there is a company out there doing 7 — just take a look at their review page that will tell its own story."*

### On business approach
> *"To get this up and running it's going to take commitment, money, and a bit of blood, sweat and tears. But there is very good coin to be made for good operators. Educate yourself first, get confident, then build it up. Rome was not built in a day."*

### On subs/training others
> *"Never teach anyone that helps you how to spray. Only teach prep work. They'll start up against you in six months."*

### On warranty for rentals
> *"In a rental, they're not going to put big warranties on a bathtub. You don't know what people are going to do in there — stick their dog in there with shark claws, do stupid things. Most guys do six months on rentals, five years on private homes."*

### On the bigger market
> *"Kitchens and benchtops is probably a bigger market than bathrooms now, and all the successful companies do all 3 [bath + tile + kitchen + benchtop]."*

### On colors
> *"99% of bathtubs are white. Tiles can be tinted but rarely requested. Custom kitchen colors any Dulux match — 2 days to mix in Melbourne, ~2 days more to ship to Sydney."*

### On methylene chloride strippers
> *"14 resurfacers killed in the US over 18 years. I won't use it. If I've got to strip a bath, I manually grind it out."*

### On competitors
- **Bathroom Werx**: bad reputation per Product Review, no contractors left, only employees
- **Thermoglaze**: good material but very expensive, none low VOC, dealer-only
- **Natco**: Bert says inferior to Hawk; many ex-Natco customers switched to Hawk

## Strategic implications for Timeless

1. **Regrouting-first is right** — no Bert dependency, can ship today

5. **Future expansion**: kitchen + benchtop are bigger market than bathroom — consider Phase 7 (year 1+)
