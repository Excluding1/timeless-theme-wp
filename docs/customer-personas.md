# Customer Personas -- Timeless Resurfacing

**Business:** Timeless Resurfacing (timelessresurfacing.com.au)
**Location:** Greater Sydney and NSW, Australia
**Services:** Bathroom resurfacing, shower regrouting, tile resurfacing, bath reglazing, vanity refinishing, basin restoration, shower leak repair
**Traffic source:** Google Ads (one service per ad group) + organic search
**Business model:** Photo-based quoting, one-day service, subcontractor network

---

## How to Use This Document

Each persona represents a real customer archetype that will land on a specific page. Use these personas to:

1. **Google Ads copywriting** -- match ad copy to the exact search queries and emotional states listed
2. **Landing page optimisation** -- ensure each page answers the specific questions and objections each persona has
3. **Quote form design** -- reduce friction by addressing trust triggers before the form
4. **Retargeting** -- understand where each persona drops off and what might bring them back
5. **Sales call preparation** -- know the objections and knowledge level before you pick up the phone

---

The homepage catches the broadest audience: people who searched for the brand, a general bathroom renovation alternative, or who landed here from a social share or referral. ## Cross-Persona Insights

### Patterns Across All 25 Personas

**1. The Knowledge Gap Is the Biggest Barrier**
Roughly half of these personas (12 of 25) do not know what resurfacing is when they first search. They use symptom-based language: "black mould won't go away," "chipped bathtub repair," "change tile colour without replacing." The landing pages MUST bridge the gap between what they searched (the problem) and what you offer (the solution) within the first scroll.

**2. Photo-Based Quoting Is a Competitive Advantage**
Nearly every persona responds positively to the photo-based quoting process. It is particularly compelling for:
- Property managers (Lisa Nguyen) who manage remotely
- Landlords (James Tran) who do not want to visit the property
- Time-poor pre-sale sellers (Priya Sharma) who are juggling too much
- Renters (Samantha Brooks) who need to send info to their landlord
- The elderly (Michelle Andersen, Angela Papas) who find forms easier when a family member helps

**3. Two Distinct Trust Triggers Exist**
- **Technical buyers** (Ryan O'Brien, Steve Morrison, Andrew Kaur) want: licence numbers, coating brand names, warranty specifics, technical process details
- **Emotional buyers** (Sarah Mitchell, Natalie Walker, Christine Demetriou) want: before/after photos of bathrooms like theirs, social proof, the "70% savings" message, reassurance it does not look fake

**4. Three Decision Speeds**
- **Urgent (1-7 days):** Property managers, Airbnb owners, people with active leaks, pre-sale sellers
- **Standard (1-4 weeks):** Most owner-occupiers with a specific problem they have finally decided to fix
- **Slow (1-4 months):** Body corporate committees, couples who need to agree, people planning around events

**5. The "Previously Burned" Segment Is Real**
Tony Moretti (Persona 3.2) represents a significant segment: people who had resurfacing done cheaply and it failed. The landing pages need to address this directly with messaging about the Hawk Glass-Tech system being different from generic spray coatings, and the warranty covering peeling and adhesion failure.

**6. The Renter-Landlord-PM Triangle**
Three personas (Samantha Brooks, Lisa Nguyen, James Tran) represent the rental property ecosystem. The renter discovers the problem, the property manager coordinates the fix, and the landlord pays. The website needs to serve all three, with the gallery page being particularly important for renters who forward it to landlords.
