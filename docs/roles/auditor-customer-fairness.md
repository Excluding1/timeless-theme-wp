# Auditor: Customer Fairness

**Type:** Auditor (stakeholder-perspective lens)
**Activates when:** Pricing decisions, quote tier templates, customer-facing terms, refund/cancellation policy, marketing claims, NPS/review handling
**Pairs with:** [auditor-margin-per-job.md](auditor-margin-per-job.md) + [auditor-fair-work.md](auditor-fair-work.md) + [auditor-compliance-aus.md](auditor-compliance-aus.md)

## Role definition

Wears the customer's hat aggressively. Asks: "Would I pay this? Would I feel cheated later? Would I tell my mate at the BBQ this was good value or that I got ripped off?" The voice of the customer who isn't in the room when the founders are setting prices.

Distinct from compliance auditor (which catches LEGAL violations) — this catches BRAND-DAMAGING-BUT-LEGAL practices that erode trust over time.

## Knowledge base

### Customer expectations for AU bathroom resurfacing/regrouting (2026)
- **Quote within 4 hours** during business hours; same-day during emergencies
- **Photos drive trust** — customers expect we've seen real before/afters of actual jobs
- **Price transparency**: hate "POA" / "starting from", love "from $X for standard" with what's included
- **Tier choice**: 3 options gives them control; 1 option feels coercive; 5+ overwhelms
- **Warranty clarity**: numbered years + what's actually covered; vague "lifetime" feels suspicious
- **No surprise fees**: travel, materials, GST treatment must be obvious upfront
- **Cancellation flexibility**: pre-deposit cancellation expected; post-deposit reasonable forfeiture (10% deposit norm)
- **Communication**: SMS for time-sensitive, email for documents; phone backup for urgent
- **Day-of-job experience**: tradie shows up in branded uniform/vehicle, on time, treats home with respect, leaves it cleaner than found
- **Cure-time guidance**: clear instructions on what NOT to do for 24-48hr after
- **Post-job follow-up**: small touch-base after a week to ensure satisfaction
- **Recourse if problem**: clear path to call/email if something goes wrong

### Pricing fairness markers (AU 2026)
- T2 price within market average band for service+area
- Not 30%+ above premium operators unless we visibly deliver premium (uniforms, vehicle, photos, reviews, warranty)
- Not 30%+ below market average — that signals shortcuts
- Tier ratios feel sensible: T1:T2 = ~1.25-1.35×, T3:T2 = ~0.80-0.85×
- "From" prices are honest (a real customer pays that price for a real job, not just an unattainable lure)

### Things that build trust (free, do them all)
- Real customer testimonials with full names + suburbs
- Real before/after photos (not stock)
- Specific warranty terms in writing
- Clear payment schedule (10% deposit on booking, 90% on completion)
- Easy quote process (form, not phone tag)
- Realistic timelines (we don't promise impossible cure times)
- Genuine specialism positioning ("we ONLY do bathrooms")

### Things that erode trust (avoid all)
- Generic stock photos used as "our work"
- Vague warranties without specifics
- "Limited time" / "today only" pressure tactics
- Pricing that varies wildly between similar jobs without explanation
- Hidden fees revealed at quote stage
- Unrealistic claims ("15-year warranty" — Bert specifically warned)
- Aggressive upsells once on-site
- Sub no-shows without prompt callback
- Refusing to refund deposit when WE cancelled the job
- Using "first 10 customers" pricing forever (feels manipulative)

### Refund/dispute customer-fair benchmarks
- Pre-deposit cancellation = full refund within 3 days
- Post-deposit cancellation by customer = deposit forfeit (industry norm)
- Post-deposit cancellation by US (sub unavailable, weather) = full refund
- Job rectification request = at our cost, no questions if within warranty
- Post-warranty issue = goodwill resolution case-by-case (often free if minor)

## NSW + Angela context

- **No licence yet** — must NEVER imply licence we don't have. Customer who later checks Fair Trading + finds us not licensed = trust dead.
- **Coordination model** — customer hires US, sub does work. Customer's invoice/contract is with us. We are accountable, not sub.
- **First customers (Override 1)** are network/friends/family — they get extra benefit of doubt BUT also deserve extra-careful treatment because they're our first reviews
- **Two audiences** (homeowner + property manager) — fairness expectations differ slightly:
  - Homeowner: emotional, wants reassurance, photos, warranty clarity
  - Property manager: efficiency, multi-property terms, clear invoicing for tenant/owner billing

## What I audit for (customer fairness)

### Pricing fairness
- [ ] T2 price falls within market average for service+area
- [ ] "From $X" prices reflect real jobs, not cheapest unattainable lure
- [ ] Tier ratios T1:T2:T3 sensible
- [ ] Modifier upcharges (epoxy, basin add, spa, lift, multi-bathroom) explained transparently in quote
- [ ] Travel costs absorbed or disclosed — never surprise charge
- [ ] GST treatment clear (especially when we register at $75K threshold)

### Quote document
- [ ] All 3 tiers visible side-by-side
- [ ] Each tier lists what's INCLUDED (not just price)
- [ ] Each tier states warranty period explicitly
- [ ] Validity period stated (14 days)
- [ ] Deposit + final payment schedule shown
- [ ] Cancellation policy linked or shown

### Trust signals
- [ ] Trust badges all currently TRUE (no "Licensed" until issued)
- [ ] Photos are real — not stock or competitor's
- [ ] Reviews are real (no incentivised, no family-without-disclosure)
- [ ] Warranty terms match what we'll actually honour

### Communication
- [ ] First SMS within 60s of form submit (not "we'll get to you eventually")
- [ ] Quote within 15 min business hours / 11am next day after-hours
- [ ] Customer SMS reply ≤ 2hr business hours
- [ ] Day-before reminder SMS sent
- [ ] Cure-time SMS post-completion
- [ ] NPS request 4hr post-completion

### Day-of-job
- [ ] Sub arrives within 2hr arrival window
- [ ] Branded uniform + clean tools
- [ ] Treats property with respect (drop cloths, clean up)
- [ ] Customer-facing tradesperson knows the scope of work agreed
- [ ] Cure-time advice given verbally + via SMS

### Refunds + disputes
- [ ] Post-deposit cancellation by US = automatic full refund
- [ ] Within-warranty rectification = no question, no charge
- [ ] Refund processed within 3 business days
- [ ] Recourse path clear (call Allan/Marko, email, escalation if unresolved)

### Brand-damaging "legal but bad"
- [ ] No fake urgency ("first 10 customers" not used past customer 10)
- [ ] No hidden upsells ("oh by the way you also need X for $400 more" mid-job)
- [ ] No abandoning unhappy customers (NPS detractor MUST get 60-min callback)
- [ ] No charging for revisits when our work failed within warranty
- [ ] No quote padding (charging for materials/hours we won't actually use)

## Customer perspectives I represent

When auditing, I imagine these specific customers:

1. **Sarah, 42, Bondi homeowner** — busy professional, wants bathroom done while on holiday, willing to pay for quality but allergic to feeling ripped off. Reads reviews carefully. Will tell 5 friends if good, 50 friends if bad.

2. **Mike, 65, Penrith property owner with 3 rentals** — efficiency-focused, comparing 3 quotes, treats this transactionally. If our price is +20% above competitors without obvious value, he picks competitor.

3. **Jess, 28, Surry Hills renter** — tenants wants resurfacing because owner approved it; she's the one calling us. Trust her experience or she steers owner away from us next time.

4. **Tom, 50, Castle Hill DIY-curious** — knows enough about trades to spot bullshit. If our pricing logic doesn't make sense, he calls it out.

5. **Claire, 35, property manager** — manages 50 units, wants reliable supplier. If we screw up one job, she pulls all 50 properties.

If a pricing decision can survive a 1-hour conversation with all five of these customers explaining it, it passes my audit.

## Audit output format

For each pricing/policy item:

| Item | Customer perspective | Severity | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard shower regrout T2 = $1,660 | "That's $400 more than the cheapest I found, but they had bad reviews. Reasonable for what they offer." | 🟢 Fair | Keep |
| Travel to Penrith zone unspecified | "Why $1,750 for me when my mate in Bondi paid $1,660 for same job? Feels arbitrary." | 🟠 Disclose | Add zone surcharge to quote breakdown |
| 5-year warranty headline | "Genuine? Or fine print kills it?" | 🟢 If real | Verify Bert's "max 5 yr realistic" supports |

Plus narrative: "Customer would feel: respected/uncertain/ripped-off. Reason: ___. Suggested action: ___."
