## Scoring framework

Each criterion scored 0-10. Total /100.

| Score | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **80-100** | PASS — approved for regular work / tier maintained |
| **60-79** | CONDITIONAL — specific feedback given; second trial / improvement window |
| **Below 60** | FAIL — do not engage / tier downgrade or removal |

### Critical-failure override (added by CEO 2026-05-01 PM)
Regardless of total score, **automatic FAIL** if any of:
- Wrong materials used (e.g., Bunnings consumer-grade silicone instead of bathroom-grade)
- Sham contracting indicator surfaces (e.g., subcontractor asks us to provide tools or supervision)
- Customer reports unprofessional behaviour (rudeness, swearing, smoking inside, etc.)
- Asbestos / WHS hazard ignored on pre-1990 job
- Subcontractor didn't take any photos
- Subcontractor didn't show up + no notice (no-show)

These trump score because they break trust + create legal/brand risk regardless of finish quality.

## A. REGROUT JOB RUBRIC

| # | Criterion | What 10/10 looks like | Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Grout lines — consistency** | Even depth + width throughout. No gaps, voids, or crumbling. Colour consistent across joints. No missed areas. | /10 |
| 2 | **Grout lines — finish** | Smooth, slightly concave profile. No rough edges. **No grout haze on tile surfaces.** Tiles perfectly clean adjacent to grout. | /10 |
| 3 | **Silicone — bead quality** | Smooth, continuous bead. Consistent width. No gaps, bubbles, or lifting at edges. Clean cut at termination points. | /10 |
| 4 | **Silicone — application** | Applied in ALL corners, wall-floor + wall-wall junctions, around fixtures. No missed junctions. Correct colour match (or white). | /10 |
| 5 | **Surface preparation** | Old grout fully removed (no remnants). Old silicone stripped completely. Surfaces clean before new application. | /10 |
| 6 | **Cleanup** | Bathroom left clean. No grout residue on floor or walls. Protective sheeting removed. Taps/fixtures wiped. Nothing left behind. | /10 |
| 7 | **Time management** | Completed within quoted timeframe. Arrived on time (within 15 min). Notified of delays proactively. | /10 |
| 8 | **Customer interaction** | Professional, friendly. Explained cure time + aftercare to customer. Customer reported positive experience. | /10 |
| 9 | **Photo documentation** | Before (full + close-ups), during (grout removal, prep), after (full + close-ups of lines + corners). All sent same day. | /10 |
| 10 | **Materials used** | Correct brand/product per spec. Grout type matches scope (cement vs epoxy). Silicone bathroom-grade + mould-resistant. **No consumer-grade substitutions.** | /10 |

## B. RESURFACE JOB RUBRIC

| # | Criterion | What 10/10 looks like | Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Coating finish — smoothness** | Completely smooth to touch. No bubbles, orange-peel, runs, sags, dust. Even gloss across surface. | /10 |
| 2 | **Coating finish — coverage** | No bare spots or thin areas where substrate shows. Consistent colour. Even film thickness. | /10 |
| 3 | **Coating finish — edges** | Clean, sharp edges at boundaries with untreated surfaces. No overspray on tiles/walls/taps/fixtures. Masking lines straight. | /10 |
| 4 | **Surface preparation** | Chips/cracks repaired + sanded smooth before coating. Surface degreased + acid-washed. Old coatings stripped if previously resurfaced. Moisture test performed. | /10 |
| 5 | **Masking + protection** | All surrounding surfaces fully protected. No overspray. Taps, drain, overflow masked. Floor protected. Towel rails + fixtures covered. | /10 |
| 6 | **Chip/crack repair** *(if applicable)* | Repairs invisible / near-invisible under final coat. No lumps, ridges, or visible filler. Smooth transitions. | /10 |
| 7 | **Cleanup** | Masking removed cleanly. No tape residue. No paint flecks on floor/walls. Bathroom clean + ready (after cure time). | /10 |
| 8 | **Ventilation + safety** | Proper ventilation set up. Respirator worn. Customer advised to ventilate 24+ hours. No chemical residue or strong odour remaining. | /10 |
| 9 | **Photo documentation** | Before (full + damage close-ups), during (prep, masking, coating stages), after (full + finish quality close-ups). All same day. | /10 |
| 10 | **Cure time instructions** | Customer given clear written + verbal: "Don't use 48-72hr. No heavy objects. Gentle cleaners only." **Failure to advise = coating fails within weeks.** | /10 |

## How to use this rubric

### For trial jobs
- Inspector (Marko) scores within 24hr of completion
- Customer feedback collected separately ([sub-trial-job-protocol.md § 5](sub-trial-job-protocol.md#step-5--customer-feedback-call-within-24-hours))
- Decision per [trial-job-protocol § 6](sub-trial-job-protocol.md#step-6--decision)

### For ongoing spot-checks
- Random audit ~1 in 10 jobs (or ~1/month per subcontractor minimum)
- Same rubric scored
- Track subcontractor's average score over rolling 90 days
- Tier movement per OPERATING-CONTEXT § 9.5:
  - 4.5+ avg (90+/100) = Tier 1
  - 3.5-4.4 (70-89) = Tier 2
  - <3.5 (<70) = warning → coaching → downgrade or removal

### For customer-complaint investigations
- When NPS detractor (1-6) reported, inspector visits + scores
- Compares to subcontractor's running avg
- Identifies if quality dropped or customer expectation issue

---

## Rubric philosophy

**This is a tool, not a punishment.** Subcontractors deserve clear standards before they're held to them. We share this rubric with subcontractors at onboarding so they know exactly how they'll be measured.

Good subcontractors welcome standards. Bad subcontractors resist measurement. Rubric is a self-selection mechanism.
