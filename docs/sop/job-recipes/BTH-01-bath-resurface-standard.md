## 1. Customer-facing summary

**Description:**
> We resurface your existing bath in white or bone using professional-grade Hawk Glass-Tech 2-part coating. Saves you replacing the bath (which costs $5,000-$15,000 with plumbing + tiling impact). Same-day work; ready to use in 48-72 hours.

**Scope (T2 Recommended tier — what's included):**
- Surface preparation (sand + degrease + clean)
- Chip + minor crack repair (up to 3 chips ≤10mm)
- 2-coat primer (Ultragrip system)
- 2-coat topcoat (Glas-Tech Lo 9100 — pure white or bone, 24hr cure)
- Masking + drop sheet protection of surrounding area
- Cleanup + cure-time guidance
- Aftercare card with care instructions
- **Up to 5-year warranty** (private home) / 6-month (rental) — workmanship + coating

**What's NOT included:**
- Plumbing work (taps, drain, overflow — must be left in place)
- Surrounding tile resurfacing (separate SKU: TWL series)
- Bath replacement
- Damage from acrylic baths flexing under heavy weight (advise customer of bath construction limits)

## 6. Step-by-step recipe

### Phase 1: Arrival (0-15min)
1. Sub texts customer "On my way — 30min" (per [customer-comms § 3C](../../templates/customer-comms-templates.md))
2. Park considerately — apartment customers warn about loading zones
3. Greet customer at door — confirm name, scope (e.g., "Standard porcelain bath, white"), tier (T1/T2/T3)
4. Photo: front of bathroom door (job-start record)
5. Walk to bathroom — assess access for spray equipment

### Phase 2: Surface assessment (5-10min)
1. Inspect bath surface — note: chip count, cracks, soft spots, previous resurface signs
2. **STOP triggers (call office immediately, do NOT proceed):**
   - Asbestos suspected (pre-1990 property + tile or surround damage)
   - >5 chips OR any chip >10mm (out of standard scope — needs requote)
   - Bath has soft/spongy area (acrylic delamination — needs replacement, not resurface)
   - Customer wants extra surfaces (wall tiles, basin) NOT in original quote
3. If clear: 3 photos of bath surface BEFORE work (full length + close-ups of damage)
4. Verbally confirm scope with customer — point to surfaces being treated; point to fixtures being masked

### Phase 3: Setup + masking (45min)
1. Lay 1 plastic drop sheet on bathroom floor
2. Lay 1 drop sheet outside bathroom door (overspray catch)
3. Mask: bath rim/wall junction, plughole, overflow, tap base, mixer body, shower head if shower-over-bath
4. Mask all surrounding tiles (3M Blue tape covers tile within 30cm of bath)
5. Open window + position extractor fan facing OUT of bathroom
6. Confirm respirator on; gloves on
7. Photo: 1 photo of masked-up bath (DURING — proves prep done)

### Phase 4: Surface prep (60min)
1. Sand bath surface with 120 grit (oscillating multi-tool with sanding pad)
2. Vacuum dust thoroughly — DO NOT just brush (residue ruins coating)
3. Wipe down with Hawk Industrial Cleaner — let dry
4. Wipe with methylated spirits — final degrease
5. Wipe with acetone — last 30 seconds before priming (eliminates oils)
6. Inspect surface — should be uniformly dull + clean

### Phase 5: Chip repair (if applicable, 15-30min)
1. Apply auto body filler to chips
2. Wait 15min cure
3. Sand chip smooth (180 grit) — must be flush with surrounding surface
4. Re-clean (methylated spirits)

### Phase 6: Primer (30min)
1. Mix Ultragrip Primer Resin + Catalyst per Hawk ratio (typically 1:1)
2. Add Reducer per humidity conditions
3. Spray Coat 1 — even passes, 50% overlap
4. Flash 15min (touch test — should be dry but tacky)
5. Spray Coat 2 — same technique
6. Flash 15min before topcoat

### Phase 7: Topcoat (60-90min)
1. Mix Glas-Tech Lo 9100 Resin + Catalyst per ratio (typically 4:1)
2. Add Reducer per humidity (~10-15%)
3. Spray Coat 1 — even passes, 50% overlap
4. Flash 15-20min
5. Spray Coat 2
6. Flash 15min
7. Spray Coat 3 (highest gloss layer, final pass) — light coat
8. Flash 30min before removing masking
9. Photo: 2 photos DURING spray (proof of technique + coverage)

### Phase 8: Quality check (10min)
1. Self-inspect against [sub-quality-rubric.md](../sub-quality-rubric.md):
   - Smooth surface, no orange peel
   - No drips or runs
   - Crisp masking edges (no overspray on tiles)
   - Even gloss
   - Full coverage (no thin spots / show-through)
2. Photo: 4 final angles — full bath, tap end, drain end, side wall view

### Phase 9: Cleanup + handover (15min)
1. Carefully remove masking while topcoat still tacky (cleaner edges)
2. Remove drop sheets — vacuum any debris
3. Clean any overspray on customer's surfaces (acetone-dampened cloth, gentle)
4. Leave Card 2 (Resurface aftercare) on vanity per [customer-aftercare-cards.md](../../templates/customer-aftercare-cards.md)
5. Walk customer through:
   - 48-72 hours NO use (water, weight, anything)
   - Use ONLY non-abrasive cleaners
   - No suction-cup mats
   - Warranty terms
6. Photo upload to ServiceM8 (8 photos: 3 before + 2 during + 4 after = NO LESS)
7. Mark job complete in SM8 — triggers Stage 11 in GHL pipeline
8. Customer SMS auto-fires: cure-time warning (W9 workflow)

## 7. Quality criteria

**Per [sub-quality-rubric.md](../sub-quality-rubric.md) — resurface 10 criteria:**

| Criterion | What's "good" (8-10) | What fails (<6) |
|---|---|---|
| Smoothness | Mirror-smooth, no orange peel | Visible orange peel, lumps, runs |
| Coverage | Full opaque white/bone, no show-through | Thin spots showing original colour |
| Edges (masking) | Crisp lines at tile junction | Overspray on tiles, fuzzy edges |
| Surface prep | No grit/dust visible in coating | Dust nibs, grit visible |
| Masking discipline | Tap/drain pristine | Coating on fixtures |
| Chip repair | Flush + invisible | Visible bump or dip at chip location |
| Cleanup | Customer area perfect | Drips on floor, overspray on towel rail |
| Time mgmt | Done within 6hr | Took >7hr (efficiency issue) |
| Customer interaction | Friendly + confident | Awkward, evasive |
| Photo documentation | All 9 photos uploaded clear | Missing or blurry photos |

**Photo requirements:**
- **Before (3):** full-length bath, close-up damage, tap end view
- **During (2):** masked-up bath (post-prep), one mid-spray
- **After (4):** full length, tap end, drain end, side angle

## 11. Warranty

**Per [customer-aftercare-cards.md Card 2](../../templates/customer-aftercare-cards.md):**

- **Private home: up to 5-year workmanship + coating guarantee**
- **Rental property: 6-month workmanship + coating** (high-traffic, multiple users)

**Includes:**
- Peeling, bubbling, discolouration not caused by improper use
- Adhesion failures
- Chip repair lift-out within warranty period

**Excludes:**
- Damage from abrasive cleaners (Ajax, Jif, steel wool, magic erasers)
- Heavy object drops (metal bottles, glass jars)
- Suction-cup bath mats (pulls coating off)
- Acetone / nail polish remover damage
- Hot objects placed directly on surface
