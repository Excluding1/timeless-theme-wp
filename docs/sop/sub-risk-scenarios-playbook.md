## Why this exists

Per Allan's request: a "scenarios situation doc" — predict what will go wrong, document the protection, define the action SOP. **Every scenario below WILL happen at some point.** The question is whether we're protected when it does.

Each scenario maps to:
- **Trigger:** what happened
- **Without protection:** what we'd lose
- **Our protection:** which clause/process/insurance covers us
- **Action SOP:** what we do, in order, when this hits

## Scenarios 1-10: Workmanship + delivery

### 1. Subcontractor does bad work (peeling, cracking, rough finish)

- **Without protection:** Customer demands full refund ($1,660). Subcontractor keeps fee ($600). We're out ~$2,260.
- **Our protection:** Clauses 5 (warranty) + 6 (rectification/deduction). Subcontractor must fix at own cost within 5 days. If they refuse, we hire another subcontractor + deduct cost.
- **Action SOP:**
  1. Customer raises issue (NPS detractor, callback, photo review flags it)
  2. Visit OR review photos to confirm
  3. Notify subcontractor in writing within 24hr; require fix within 5 business days
  4. If subcontractor refuses → hire alternate subcontractor, deduct cost from subcontractor's outstanding invoices
  5. Document the failure for subcontractor's quarterly review + scorecard (Stage 1 verbal warning)
  6. Update customer with timeline; offer goodwill (e.g., free silicone reseal at 6mo) if they had to wait

### 2. Subcontractor no-shows on the day

- **Without protection:** Customer waited all day. 1-star Google review. Scrambling to reschedule.
- **Our protection:** Clause 12(b) immediate termination for material breach. Clause 32 committed-job cancellation = $100 fee + warning. Day-before reminder SMS sets expectation.
- **Action SOP:**
  1. Subcontractor doesn't arrive within 30min of booked time → call subcontractor; if no response within 15min →
  2. Call customer immediately: "We need to reschedule — we're really sorry, this isn't normal" (DO NOT blame the subcontractor publicly)
  3. Offer earliest reschedule + small goodwill ($50 off, free silicone, etc)
  4. Issue Clause 32 fee deduction to no-show subcontractor; written warning regardless
  5. 3rd no-show in 6 months → termination per Clause 12

### 3. Subcontractor steals customer (direct work, business card)

- **Without protection:** Subcontractor gives card; customer calls them next time. We lose lead cost + LTV.
- **Our protection:** Clause 9 (non-solicitation, 12 months); Clause 26h (no business cards / direct selling). Enforceable because narrowed to personally-serviced customers (UCT compliant).
- **Action SOP:**
  1. Customer mentions "your guy gave me his card" or subcontractor directly poaches
  2. Document evidence (customer message screenshot, ABN search of subcontractor's website)
  3. Written notice to subcontractor citing Clause 9 breach
  4. Decision: terminate immediately (recommended) OR demand 6-month exclusion + monitoring
  5. If subcontractor continues poaching → small claims for breach (Clause 9 damages clause)
  6. **Customer side:** call customer, explain "we manage all our work directly; if you'd like to keep working with us, here's what we offer" — don't shame the customer, win them back on service

### 4. Subcontractor injures themselves on site

- **Without protection:** Sole trader with no insurance may sue or claim against our PL. $100K+ claim.
- **Our protection:** Vetting #3 (PL insurance ≥$10M), #4 (personal accident); Clause 8 (insurance mandatory); Clause 1 (independent contractor); WHS obligations (Clause 14).
- **Action SOP:**
  1. Subcontractor injured → ensure medical care first
  2. Get incident report from subcontractor within 48hrs (date, time, mechanism, photos of scene)
  3. Notify our PL insurer for awareness (no claim yet)
  4. If subcontractor claims employee status → engage employment lawyer; produce contract + 9-indicator audit + dispatch records (proves contractor status)
  5. Document everything in subcontractor file

### 5. Subcontractor damages customer property (overspray, chemical spill, broken tile)

- **Without protection:** Customer demands compensation FROM US. Could be $500-$10,000+.
- **Our protection:** Subcontractor's PL insurance covers; Clause 8 (insurance mandatory); Clause 25 (subcontractor indemnifies for damage); Clause 26f (cleanup standards).
- **Action SOP:**
  1. Customer reports damage → visit + photo within 24hrs
  2. Estimate repair cost (3 quotes if >$500)
  3. Notify subcontractor: "Your work caused [damage]. Per Clause 25, you indemnify. Provide claim through your PL insurer within 5 business days."
  4. We pay customer to maintain trust (customer relationship is ours); recover from subcontractor via insurance
  5. If subcontractor's PL is lapsed/insufficient → we pay; small claims to recover from subcontractor

### 6. Customer claims asbestos exposure

- **Without protection:** Pre-1990 property, subcontractor disturbed asbestos. $50K+ fine + health liability.
- **Our protection:** Quote form captures property age (Year Built field); Clause 19 (stop work, never disturb); Clause 14 (asbestos awareness training cert required); rejection criteria for suspected asbestos properties pre-quote.
- **Action SOP:**
  1. Customer or subcontractor reports asbestos suspicion → STOP WORK immediately
  2. Engage licensed asbestos assessor ($300-500) within 48hrs
  3. If asbestos confirmed → engage licensed asbestos removalist; do NOT dispatch our subcontractor for the removal
  4. Reschedule resurface for after asbestos cleared + air-quality-test passed
  5. Document everything in customer + subcontractor file
  6. Per [auditor-compliance-aus.md](../roles/auditor-compliance-aus.md): SafeWork NSW notification may be required if disturbance occurred — check thresholds with WHS adviser

### 7-10. (Compliance-adjacent — sham contracting, ATO, materials, non-payment)

## Scenarios 11-20: Behaviour + brand

### 11. Subcontractor uses wrong materials despite contract

- Resurface peels in 3 months → consumer guarantee liability; full re-do $800-1,200
- **Protection:** Clause 4 + Clause 5 + photo review (check product brand visible in before/during photos); Clause 12 (unapproved materials = warning then termination)
- **SOP:** Photo review identifies wrong product → same-day written notice; require switch + cite Clause 4; if next job repeats → Stage 2 written warning; 3rd time → termination

### 12. Subcontractor poaches customer (cheap-direct offer)

- Lose customer + future referrals; subcontractor builds competing business using our leads
- **Protection:** Clause 9, 10, 26g+h
- **SOP:** Per Scenario 3 above. Plus: brief Marko on standard rebuttal — "we manage all our work directly; that's how we maintain consistency and warranty"

### 13. Subcontractor posts customer photos on own social media

- Privacy Act 1988 breach exposure; customer complains
- **Protection:** Clause 20 (IP/photo licence to us); Clause 23 (data/privacy); onboarding briefing (Step 13 of [sub-onboarding-checklist](sub-onboarding-checklist.md))
- **SOP:** Customer reports → screenshot evidence → request immediate removal from subcontractor within 24hr; Stage 1 verbal warning → if repeated, Stage 2 written; 3rd → termination

### 14. Subcontractor damages property beyond work area (scratched tiles, cracked tap, stained carpet)

- Customer demands compensation; if subcontractor's insurance lapsed, we're exposed
- **Protection:** Clause 8 (insurance $10M PL); Clause 25 (mutual indemnity); Clause 26e (restricted areas); insurance expiry tracking ([sub-ongoing-quality-monitoring.md § 8](sub-ongoing-quality-monitoring.md))
- **SOP:** Per Scenario 5

### 15. Subcontractor injured + claims employment status (workers comp)

- If reclassified: workers comp + back-pay + super + payroll tax → potentially $50K+
- **Protection:** Clause 1 (contractor); Clause 8 (personal accident insurance encouraged); sham protections; Pty Ltd subcontractors = bulletproof (super/comp obligations are the company's, not ours)
- **SOP:** Per Scenario 4 + 7

### 16. Unfair contract term (UCT) challenge from subcontractor or subcontractor's lawyer

- Clause declared void; penalty up to $50M per contravention (realistically: clause struck out)
- **Protection:** Clause 22 (UCT compliance, Sprintlaw review); most-likely targets (Clause 6 deductions, Clause 9 non-solicitation, Clause 12 termination) all already narrowed for UCT compliance
- **SOP:** Engage Sprintlaw same week; produce drafting history; if struck out, replace with narrower wording + apply prospectively to existing subcontractors; document for [auditor-compliance-aus](../roles/auditor-compliance-aus.md) quarterly review

### 17. Subcontractor works for competitor + takes our systems/pricing/customer list

- Competitor undercuts us with our rate card; contacts our customers directly
- **Protection:** Clause 10 (confidentiality survives termination); Clause 9 non-solicitation; **don't share full customer DB with subcontractors — only job-specific info**
- **SOP:** Document evidence (rate-card screenshot, customer report); demand cease-and-desist via lawyer letter ($300-500); small claims for breach if quantifiable damage

### 18. Subcontractor asks customer how much they're paying

- Subcontractor discovers our 50-70% markup; gets resentful, demands rates or poaches
- **Protection:** Clause 10 pricing prohibition; Clause 26g "all pricing goes through the office"; onboarding briefing — scripted response
- **SOP:** Subcontractor reports awkward customer question → script: "All pricing is handled by the office, mate — easier for everyone." Customer asks subcontractor directly → Marko follows up with customer same day to redirect.

### 19. Subcontractor arrives late (1-2hr, not full no-show)

- Customer took time off; annoyed; if repeated, no rebook/referral
- **Protection:** Clause 26b (notify if >15min delayed); day-before reminder SMS reduces this
- **SOP:** Subcontractor notifies on time → Marko notifies customer with apology + ETA → soft warning to subcontractor. Subcontractor doesn't notify → Stage 1 verbal. 3rd lateness → Stage 2 written.

### 20. Subcontractor arrives under influence / unprofessional

- Customer refuses entry OR subcontractor does bad work impaired; safety risk
- **Protection:** Clause 26c (zero tolerance); Clause 14 WHS
- **SOP:** Marko (or whoever's contacted) → stand subcontractor down immediately, don't accuse on-site, reschedule customer with explanation "tech unavailable today, rescheduling"; formal meeting with subcontractor within 24hrs; confirmed = immediate termination per Clause 12(b)

## How to use this playbook

1. **Crisis triggers:** when one of these scenarios fires, find it here, apply the action SOP. **Do not improvise** — improvisation is what gets us into NCAT or Fair Work disputes.
2. **Quarterly review:** [auditor-general-operational](../roles/auditor-general-operational.md) reviews this list against actual incidents this quarter — has any scenario fired more than twice? If yes → preventive process change (e.g., scenario 24 cancellations >10% → review confirmation flow).
3. **New scenarios:** when a new pattern emerges (e.g., a new failure mode we hadn't anticipated), add scenario #29 + protect with new clause if needed.
