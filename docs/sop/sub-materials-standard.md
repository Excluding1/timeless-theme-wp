# SOP: Approved Materials & Equipment Standard for Subcontractors

**Purpose:** Quick-reference checklist for subcontractor conversations. Subcontractor says "I use brand X" → we look here → approve or escalate.

**Owner:** Marko (subcontractor conversations) + CEO (list maintenance)

**Status:** ⚠️ STARTER SCAFFOLD — brands marked ❓ pending real-Sydney verification via pricing audit. Use with caution; if a brand isn't on this list, escalate to CEO+Marko BEFORE approving.

**Last updated:** 2026-05-01 PM (scaffold only)

---

## How to use this SOP

### When onboarding a subcontractor
1. Walk through this list with the subcontractor
2. Confirm they use brands marked ✅ approved
3. If they use a brand marked ❓ pending, note it for CEO research
4. If they use a brand NOT on this list, escalate to CEO+Marko before approving

### When subcontractor asks during a job ("is X brand OK?")
1. Look up category
2. Find brand on approved list → ✅ approve immediately
3. Brand NOT on list → text Marko + CEO before approving. Apply quality criteria below if no time:
   - Is it from a recognised AU/global trades brand?
   - Does it meet relevant Australian Standard (AS/NZS)?
   - Has the subcontractor used it successfully on prior jobs?
   - Does packaging look like trade-grade (not consumer-grade)?

### Quality principle
**We don't care which brand. We care that the brand makes good results.** Cheap product = bad job = bad review = brand damage. Splurge on quality even for "disposable" items.

## What NOT to use (universal avoid list)

❌ **Methylene chloride strippers** — Bert: "killed 14 resurfacers in US over 18 years"
❌ **Generic dollar-shop silicone** — yellows in months
❌ **Plastic drop sheets only** — slippery, tear easily
❌ **Latex gloves** — allergen + lower chemical resistance
❌ **Disposable paper dust masks for spraying** — inadequate
❌ **Domestic vacuums** — filters clog, motors burn
❌ **Consumer-tier Wagner-style HVLP** — burns out fast
❌ **Cheap "no-name" grout** — fails within 1-2 years
❌ **Generic tan masking tape** — bleeds on wet surfaces
❌ **Cheap painter's tape (sub-$3)** — residue or no-clean removal

## CEO research mandate (when validating this list)

When the pricing audit research happens (next session), each brand entry needs:
- [ ] Verified availability in Sydney (online or in-store)
- [ ] Verified current price (within ±15% of estimate)
- [ ] Cross-checked at least 1 subcontractor or supplier review
- [ ] Removed if discontinued or quality has slipped per recent reviews

### Specific research channels (per Allan 2026-05-01 PM)

1. **Facebook AU trades groups** — Sydney Tradies, NSW Tiles & Bathroom Renovations, Australian Tradies Network. Search for "best silicone for bathroom" / "best grout removal blade" / "best HVLP turbine for bathroom resurfacing". Filter for posts with high engagement from established operators.

2. **Reddit AU communities** — r/AusTrades, r/sydney, r/AusFinance (for cost references). Look for posts from people with verified trade backgrounds. Read comment threads for nuanced advice.

3. **Competitor websites — FAQ + materials sections**
   - Many AU resurfacing/regrouting businesses publicly list products they use in their FAQ
   - Example searches: "[competitor name] uses what materials", "[competitor name] Hawk products", "Sydney bathroom resurfacing brand"
   - Specific competitors to scan: Bathroom Werx (despite reputation), Surface Care (Jordan's), Thermoglaze dealers, smaller Sydney resurfacing operators visible in Google search

4. **Hipages + Airtasker reviews** — read reviews for established Sydney operators. They often mention brands when describing job quality or issues.

5. **Bert's existing network** — when Marko's resurfacing subcontractor network is engaged, ASK them directly: "What brands do you use and trust?"

6. **Bunnings + Total Tools customer reviews** — for disposables and tools, Bunnings reviews from verified tradespeople are gold.

### Research protocol per category
When validating a brand entry on this list:
1. Search 3 of the above channels minimum
2. Find at least 5 mentions from established operators (not opinions, not marketing)
3. Cross-check that no recent quality issues / recalls exist
4. Confirm AU availability + Sydney supply chain
5. Mark ✅ approved with confidence rating + sources cited

If a brand doesn't pass this filter, mark ❌ avoid and move to the avoid list.
