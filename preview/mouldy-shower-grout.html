Mouldy grout isn’t just ugly — it’s a problem that gets worse every week.

Health Risk

Mould spores released in shower steam irritate airways, trigger allergies, and can cause respiratory issues — especially for children and the elderly.

Cleaning Doesn’t Work

Bleach only whitens the surface. Mould roots grow deep inside porous cement grout and regrow within weeks. You’re fighting a losing battle.

Mouldy Grout FAQs

Common questions about mouldy shower grout.

Mould grows inside porous cement grout, not just on the surface. Cleaning products only remove the visible surface layer — the roots remain embedded in the grout and regrow within weeks. The only permanent solution is to remove and replace all the grout.

Most standard showers are completed in a single day (4–6 hours). Your shower is ready to use again after 24 hours to allow the new grout to cure.

Both are available, but we strongly recommend epoxy grout for mould-prone showers. Epoxy is 100% waterproof and non-porous, so mould physically cannot grow inside it. Cement grout is porous and will eventually develop mould again.

With proper regrouting using mould-resistant grout (especially epoxy), the problem is solved at the source. We remove all contaminated grout, treat the area with anti-mould solution, and replace with grout that mould cannot penetrate.

No — only the grout between tiles is removed. Your tiles stay in place. We use precision oscillating tools to carefully extract old grout without damaging any tiles.
