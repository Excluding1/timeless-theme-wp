The Science

Why Your Silicone Keeps Going Black

It’s not a cleaning problem. It’s a material science problem. Here’s the 3-step failure cycle:

1

Constant Moisture + Poor Ventilation

Shower corners and junctions are the wettest parts of your bathroom. They get drenched daily and rarely dry fully — especially in enclosed showers with limited airflow. This creates the perfect environment for mould spores.

2

Mould Grows INTO the Silicone Body

Silicone is microscopically porous. Mould spores don’t just sit on the surface — they colonise inside the silicone itself, sending root-like structures (hyphae) deep into the material. This is why the black staining goes right through.

3

Cleaning Only Kills the Surface — Roots Survive

Bleach and mould sprays kill the visible mould on the surface, but the root network inside the silicone is untouched. Within 2-4 weeks, the mould regrows from within. This cycle repeats until the silicone is physically removed and replaced.

warning The only permanent fix: strip the old silicone out completely and replace it with fresh, mould-resistant sealant.
