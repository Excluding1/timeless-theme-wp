The Science

Why Your Floor Grout Won’t Come Clean

It’s not that you’re not cleaning hard enough. It’s 4 factors working against you:

1

Foot Traffic Grinds Dirt In

Every step on your bathroom floor pushes dirt, oils, skin cells, and soap residue into the porous grout surface. This happens hundreds of times a day — far more than wall grout ever experiences.

2

Cement Grout Absorbs Everything

Cement-based grout is porous. It absorbs water, dirt, body oils, soap scum, and cleaning chemicals. Over time, these substances penetrate deep into the grout body where surface cleaning can’t reach.

3

Cleaning Doesn’t Penetrate Deep Enough

Even industrial-strength cleaners only affect the top millimetre of grout. The staining that’s been building up for years goes much deeper. You get a temporary improvement that darkens again within days.

4

Grout Sits LOWER Than Tiles — Collects Everything

Floor grout lines are recessed below the tile surface, creating tiny channels that collect water, dirt, and debris. Even mopping pushes dirty water into these channels rather than removing it.

warning Once staining penetrates below the surface, the only lasting fix is to remove and replace the grout entirely.

Cleaning vs Regrouting — Which Do You Need?

The right approach depends on how deep the damage goes:

Feature Professional Clean Full Regrout
When It Works Surface staining only, grout structurally sound Deep staining, cracks, crumbling, or repeated cleaning failures
What It Involves Deep clean with professional equipment, then seal Remove all old grout, clean joints, apply fresh grout, seal
Longevity Months to 1-2 years before staining returns 8-10 years (cement) or 15-20 years (epoxy)
Best For Newer grout with light surface staining Permanently stained, aged, or damaged grout

star Cement vs Epoxy for Floors

For high-traffic wet areas (bathrooms, laundries), epoxy grout is the premium choice:

Cement Grout

  • + Lower cost
  • − Absorbs stains over time
  • − Needs sealing every 1-2 years

Epoxy Grout Best for floors

  • + 100% stain-proof
  • + Never needs sealing
  • + Lasts 15-20 years

Our 6-step process for lasting, clean floor grout.

Assess Floor Condition

Floor Tile Regrouting FAQs

Common questions about floor grout replacement.

Floor grout takes more abuse than any other grout in your home. Daily foot traffic grinds dirt, oils, and soap scum into the porous cement surface. Because grout lines sit lower than the tiles, they collect everything that washes across the floor. Over months and years, this staining penetrates deep into the grout body where surface cleaning can’t reach.

If the staining is only surface-level and the grout is structurally sound (no cracks, no crumbling), a professional clean and seal might work. But if you’ve already tried cleaning multiple times without lasting results, or if the grout is cracked and deteriorating, full regrouting is the lasting solution. We can assess from photos and recommend the right approach.

Most bathroom or laundry floor regrouts are completed in a single day (4-8 hours depending on the floor area and tile size). The new grout needs 24 hours to cure before you can walk on it. We’ll advise on the expected timeframe in your quote.

The floor needs to be clear for us to work. Bathroom fixtures like the toilet and vanity stay in place — we work around them. Loose items (rugs, bins, scales, etc.) should be removed. For laundry or kitchen floors, moveable appliances may need to be shifted temporarily — we’ll advise when quoting.

Yes — we carry a wide range of grout colours and can match your existing colour or choose something new. Many customers take the opportunity to switch to a lighter colour for a brighter, cleaner look. With epoxy grout, the colour is permanent and won’t darken over time.

For high-traffic wet areas (bathrooms, laundries), epoxy grout is an excellent choice. It’s 100% waterproof, stain-proof, and never needs sealing. For lower-traffic areas like hallways or living rooms, cement grout with a quality sealer is often sufficient and more cost-effective. We’ll recommend the best option based on your specific situation.
