Getting Started

Resurfacing is a process where we apply a new, durable coating over your existing bathtub, tiles, basin, or vanity. Instead of ripping everything out and starting from scratch (which costs $25K-$50K and takes weeks), we prep the surface, repair any chips or damage, and spray on commercial-grade two-part epoxy coatings. The result looks and feels like a brand-new surface. It lasts up to 10 years and saves you up to 70% compared to a full renovation. No demolition, no plumber, no tiler — just one team, in and out.

It takes about 2 minutes. Use the quote form on our website — snap a few photos of your bathroom on your phone, tell us what needs work, and we’ll send you a fixed-price quote by the next business day. You can also call us on 0451 110 154. No obligation, no call-out fee. The price we quote is the price you pay.

We cover everything to do with refreshing your bathroom without a full renovation: bath resurfacing (chipped, stained, or dated bathtubs), tile resurfacing (wall and floor tiles), shower regrouting (full grout removal and replacement), shower sealing (silicone replacement and leak repair), epoxy grout upgrades (waterproof, mould-resistant grout), vanity refinishing (benchtop and cabinet respray), basin restoration (chip repair and full resurface), and full bathroom makeover packages that combine multiple services at a package price.

All of Greater Sydney — Sydney CBD, Eastern Suburbs, Inner West, North Shore, Northern Beaches, Western Sydney, Parramatta, Hills District, and Sutherland Shire. We also service Wollongong, the Central Coast, and the Blue Mountains. If you’re not sure whether we cover your area, just ask — chances are we do.

For most people, yes. Materials & Safety

We use commercial-grade two-part epoxy and polyurethane coating systems — the same products used by professional resurfacers across Australia. They’re specifically designed for wet environments: waterproof, heat-resistant, chemical-resistant, and UV-stable so they won’t yellow over time. For regrouting, we use premium cement-based or epoxy grout depending on the application.

Cement grout is the standard — it’s cheaper, easier to work with, and suitable for most applications. But it’s porous, meaning it absorbs water and grows mould over time. Epoxy grout costs more but is completely waterproof and mould-resistant. It doesn’t need sealing and lasts significantly longer. We recommend epoxy for shower areas and anywhere that gets consistently wet. We’ll advise which is best for your situation.

If your home was built before 1990, there’s a chance some materials may contain asbestos — particularly in wall sheeting behind tiles or in old adhesives. NSW regulations may require an asbestos assessment before certain work can begin. We’ll flag this during quoting and advise if a check is needed. Safety is non-negotiable. We follow all NSW WorkSafe and Fair Trading requirements.

Once fully cured (24 hours), the surface is completely safe — it’s inert and non-toxic. During application, we do use coatings that produce fumes, so we ask that kids and pets stay out of the work area and ideally in another part of the house. We ventilate thoroughly and the fumes clear within a few hours of finishing. After the 24-hour cure, it’s safe for everyone.
