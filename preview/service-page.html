Epoxy vs Cement Grout

We offer both, but for showers we almost always recommend epoxy. Here’s why:

construction Cement-Based Grout

  • + Lower upfront cost
  • + Easier colour matching
  • − Porous — absorbs water and stains
  • − Requires periodic sealing
  • − Prone to mould growth
  • − Lifespan: 8-10 years

star Epoxy Grout Recommended

  • + 100% waterproof — no absorption
  • + Stain-proof and chemical resistant
  • + Mould and mildew resistant
  • + Never needs sealing
  • + Lifespan: 15-20 years
  • − Higher upfront cost (+$250)

Shower Regrouting FAQs

Common questions about shower regrouting in Sydney.

Shower regrouting starts from $1,080 inc GST for a standard shower with cement grout. Epoxy grout upgrades add approximately $250. We provide fixed-price quotes based on your photos — no hidden fees, no surprises.

Most shower regrouting is completed in 4-6 hours. Your shower is ready to use again 24 hours after completion to allow the new grout to fully cure.

Epoxy grout is waterproof, stain-resistant, and never needs sealing — it lasts 15-20 years. Cement grout is cheaper but porous, requires sealing, and lasts 8-10 years. We recommend epoxy for showers because of constant water exposure.

In many cases, yes. Cracked or missing grout is one of the most common causes of shower leaks. Regrouting with waterproof epoxy seals the joints and prevents water penetration. If the leak is from a failed waterproofing membrane, additional repairs may be needed — we’ll advise during the quote.

If your tiles are still in good condition (not cracked or loose), regrouting is the better option. It costs up to 80% less than retiling, takes hours instead of days, and produces no demolition waste.
