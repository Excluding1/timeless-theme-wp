**R2 C2**: PAID TRIAL JOB — THE QUALITY GATE

**R3 C2**: No sub gets regular work until they pass a paid trial job that YOU inspect. This is the #1 most important quality control step. Skip it and you're gambling your business on trust.

**R5 C2**: Step

**R5 C3**: What Happens

**R5 C4**: Notes

**R6 C2**: 1. SELECT A
TRIAL JOB

**R6 C3**: Pick a REAL customer job that matches the sub's claimed skills.

For regrout: a standard shower regrout + silicone.
For resurface: a standard bath resurface.

DO NOT use a practice piece or your own bathroom. A real job under real conditions (time pressure, customer expectations, unfamiliar bathroom) reveals true skill.

**R6 C4**: Pick a job that's straightforward — not a complex multi-area job. You want to isolate their core skill.

Tell the customer: "Our technician is new to our team and we'll be quality-checking the job ourselves."

**R7 C2**: 2. BRIEF THE SUB

**R7 C3**: Send them the job brief (same as any regular job):
• Customer name + address
• Service required
• Photos from the customer's submission
• Expected completion time
• Materials to use (specify Hawk Glass-Tech for resurface, or approved grout brand for regrout)

Also tell them:
"I'll be visiting the site to inspect the completed work before I confirm final payment."

**R7 C4**: Set the expectation upfront that you're inspecting. Good subs welcome this. Bad subs get nervous.

**R8 C2**: 3. SUB COMPLETES
THE JOB

**R8 C3**: Sub arrives on time, does the work, takes before/during/after photos, sends you the photos via ServiceM8 or Slack.

You are NOT on site during the work (you're not supervising — that creates sham contracting risk). You inspect AFTER.

**R8 C4**: ⚠️ SHAM CONTRACTING: Do NOT stand over them and direct their work. You're a principal contractor, not a supervisor. You set the scope and quality standard. They choose how to achieve it.

Being on site to inspect the finished result is fine.

**R9 C2**: 4. YOU INSPECT
(within 24 hours)

**R9 C3**: Visit the site after the job is complete (or on the same day if nearby). Use the QUALITY SCORING RUBRIC (Sheet 04) to assess.

Inspect:
• Grout lines (straight, consistent, no gaps, no haze on tiles)
• Silicone beads (smooth, even, no lifting, correct colour)
• Coating finish (smooth, even colour, no bubbles, no runs, no dust)
• Masking (no overspray, no damage to surrounding surfaces)
• Cleanup (bathroom left clean, no debris, protective sheeting removed)
• Customer interaction (ask customer: "How was the technician?")

Take your own photos of the finished work.

**R9 C4**: Score using the rubric on Sheet 04.

PASS = 80%+ score → approved for regular work.
CONDITIONAL = 60-79% → specific feedback given, second trial required.
FAIL = below 60% → do not engage. Pay for the trial job and move on.

**R10 C2**: 5. CUSTOMER FEEDBACK

**R10 C3**: Call the customer within 24 hours of job completion.

Ask:
1. "How was the technician — on time, professional, friendly?"
2. "Are you happy with the result?"
3. "Was the bathroom left clean?"
4. "Anything that could have been better?"

This is separate from your visual inspection. Sometimes work looks perfect but the sub was rude, late, or left a mess.

**R10 C4**: Record feedback against the sub's profile.

If customer is unhappy → investigate. It might be the sub's fault (fix + don't hire) or the customer's expectations (manage expectations better next time).

**R11 C2**: 6. DECISION

**R11 C3**: PASS → Sign the subcontractor agreement (Sheet 05). Add to your active sub roster. Start dispatching regular jobs.

CONDITIONAL PASS → Give specific written feedback ("Your grout lines were good but the silicone bead was inconsistent in corners — here's what we expect [photo reference]"). Assign one more trial job.

FAIL → Pay them for the trial job (you owe them for the work done). Thank them. Do not engage further. Move on to the next candidate.

**R11 C4**: ⚠️ You MUST pay for the trial job regardless of pass/fail. They did the work, you owe them. Not paying creates legal and reputation problems.

Budget for 2-3 trial jobs per sub category before you find good subs. At $400-$600 per trial, that's $1,200-$1,800 investment in quality — cheap insurance against $10K+ in callbacks and refunds.

**R12 C2**: 7. SECOND TRIAL
(if conditional)

**R12 C3**: Same process but with specific feedback from trial #1 to see if they improved. If they hit 80%+ on trial #2 → approve. If still below → don't engage.

Maximum 2 trials per sub. If they can't pass in 2 attempts, they're not right for your standard.

**R12 C4**: Some subs are good but sloppy until they understand your standard. The second trial catches these — they're often your best long-term subs because they showed they can adapt.
