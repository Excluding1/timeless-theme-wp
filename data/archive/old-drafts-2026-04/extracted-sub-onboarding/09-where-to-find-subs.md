**R2 C2**: WHERE TO FIND SUBS — RECRUITMENT CHANNELS

**R4 C2**: Channel

**R4 C3**: How To Use It

**R5 C2**: Gumtree +
Facebook Marketplace

**R5 C3**: Post: "Experienced bathroom regrout/resurfacing technician wanted — subcontractor basis"

Include: ABN required, own tools + materials, public liability insurance, Sydney metro, flat rate per job.

This is where most sole-trader tradies look for work. Expect 10-20 responses. 80% won't pass vetting.

**R6 C2**: Facebook Groups
(trade-specific)

**R6 C3**: Join: "Sydney Tilers," "Bathroom Renovation Australia," "Aussie Tradies," "Subcontractors NSW."

Post or look for people advertising their services. DM promising candidates.

Trade Facebook groups are the #1 source for niche subs like regrout specialists.

**R7 C2**: Seek / Indeed
(contractor listings)

**R7 C3**: Post as a contractor opportunity, not employment. Make it clear: ABN required, independent contractor, flat rate.

Wording matters for sham contracting protection — don't use "hiring" or "employee." Use "engaging subcontractors" or "seeking independent contractors."

**R8 C2**: Hipages / Airtasker
(screen existing tradies)

**R8 C3**: Search for regrout/resurfacing providers on these platforms. Look at their reviews, response times, and portfolio.

Approach them directly: "We coordinate bathroom resurfacing work across Sydney and are looking for experienced subs to add to our network. Interested?"

**R9 C2**: ARS (Australasian
Resurfacing Supplies)

**R9 C3**: Your coating supplier. ARS sells Hawk Glass-Tech and knows who's buying it = they know who's doing resurfacing work in Sydney.

Ask: "Do you know any experienced resurfacing technicians in Sydney who might be looking for additional work?"

**R10 C2**: Existing sub's
network

**R10 C3**: Once you have one good sub, ask them: "Know anyone else who does quality work and might want sub work?"

Good tradies know other good tradies. This is often the best source of quality subs.

**R11 C2**: Jim's / Surface Care
/ Bathroom Werx
alumni

**R11 C3**: People who've worked for franchise models understand the dispatch model. They're already trained. They left because they want more flexibility or better rates.

Approach carefully — don't poach active subs (bad reputation). Look for people who've recently left or are doing independent work.

**R12 C2**: Trade schools /
TAFE

**R12 C3**: For regrout subs: recently qualified tilers looking for experience. They know tile/grout fundamentals.

For resurface subs: automotive spray painters transitioning to bathroom work. The spray application skills transfer directly.

Higher training investment, but they're mouldable to your quality standard.
