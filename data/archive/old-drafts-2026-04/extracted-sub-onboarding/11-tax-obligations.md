**R7 C2**: What is TPAR?

**R7 C3**: A yearly report to the ATO listing every payment you made to subcontractors for building/construction services during the financial year. The ATO cross-references this with your subs' tax returns to check they're reporting all income.

**R7 C4**: You MUST lodge this. It's not optional.

**R8 C2**: Does it apply to you?

**R8 C3**: YES. Your business pays subcontractors for bathroom regrouting and resurfacing = building and construction maintenance. If 50%+ of your business income comes from construction services (yours is 100%), you must lodge a TPAR.

**R8 C4**: Applies from your first financial year of operation.

**R9 C2**: What you report
for each sub

**R9 C3**: • Sub's ABN
• Sub's name and address
• Total gross amount paid during the financial year (inc GST)
• GST component of that total

You get all of this from their Tax Invoices.

**R9 C4**: Keep every Tax Invoice from every sub. Xero tracks this automatically if set up correctly.

**R10 C2**: Deadline

**R10 C3**: 28 August each year for the financial year that ended 30 June.

E.g., for payments made 1 July 2026 – 30 June 2027, lodge by 28 August 2027.

**R10 C4**: Set a calendar reminder for 1 August each year: "Prepare TPAR."

**R11 C2**: How to lodge

**R11 C3**: Electronically via:
• ATO Business Portal (online)
• Your Xero/MYOB/QuickBooks accounting software (most have TPAR export)
• Your accountant lodges on your behalf

Xero has a built-in TPAR report — it pulls data from your sub supplier payments automatically.

**R11 C4**: Ask your accountant to set up TPAR reporting in Xero from day one. Much easier than reconstructing records later.

**R12 C2**: Penalties for
not lodging

**R12 C3**: Failure to lodge on time: $313 per 28-day period (up to $1,565). But the real risk is the ATO flags you for audit and discovers sham contracting or unreported income. The TPAR is how the ATO caught contractors who weren't meeting obligations — 185,000 businesses and $507 billion in payments in 2024-25.

**R12 C4**: Lodge on time. Every year. No exceptions.

**R13 C3**: If a sub doesn't provide an ABN, you MUST withhold 47% tax from their payments (top marginal rate). This is called "no ABN withholding."

In practice: don't engage subs without an ABN. It's a mandatory vetting check (Sheet 02, item #1).

**R13 C4**: ABN verification is already in your vetting checklist. If they can't produce one, don't engage them.

ATO 3-part test: (1) contract mainly for labour — FAILS: sub supplies $50-$100 materials + tools + vehicle. (2) Paid for personal labour, not a result — FAILS: flat rate per completed job. (3) Cannot delegate — FAILS: contract includes delegation right.

**R19 C2**: SUB OPERATING
THROUGH A
COMPANY (PTY LTD)

**R19 C3**: If the sub invoices through their own Pty Ltd company (not as a sole trader), super is generally NOT required.

This is because the super guarantee applies to individuals, not companies. The company employs the individual — the company is responsible for their super.

Some subs set up a Pty Ltd specifically for this reason.

**R19 C4**: ✅ GENERALLY NO — super not required if invoicing through a company.

Confirm: check their ABN on ABR. If entity type = "Company" (not "Individual/Sole Trader"), super is the company's obligation, not yours.

Still get accountant confirmation.

**R20 C2**: WHAT IF YOU
DON'T PAY SUPER
AND YOU SHOULD HAVE?

**R20 C3**: Super Guarantee Charge (SGC) = the super you should have paid + nominal interest (10% per annum) + $20 admin fee per sub per quarter.

SGC is NOT tax deductible (unlike super itself, which is).

Plus potential Part 7 penalty: up to 200% of the SGC amount.

Plus: the ATO actively cross-references TPAR data with super reporting to find businesses that pay contractors but don't pay super.

**R20 C4**: ⚠️ THE PENALTY IS WORSE THAN THE COST.

If in doubt, pay super. 12% is a known cost. The SGC + penalties + non-deductibility is FAR more expensive.

Current super rate: 12% (FY 2025-26, effective 1 July 2025). This is the final scheduled increase.

Pay via a super clearing house (e.g., ATO Small Business Super Clearing House — free). Pay quarterly by the due dates: 28 Oct, 28 Jan, 28 Apr, 28 Jul.

**R22 C2**: C. PORTABLE LONG SERVICE LEAVE (NSW)

**R23 C2**: NSW has a portable long service leave scheme for building/construction workers, administered by the Long Service Corporation.

Key question: does bathroom regrouting/resurfacing count as "building and construction work" under the Building and Construction Industry Long Service Payments Act 1986?

The Act covers: "Any work carried out on a building site by a worker in a contract of service/training." It specifically includes: maintenance, repair, renovation, painting, decorating, finishing work.

Regrouting and resurfacing could arguably fall within "maintenance, repair, finishing work."

HOWEVER: Your subs are independent contractors, not "workers in a contract of service." The scheme primarily applies to employees. Genuine independent contractors are generally NOT covered.

⚠️ ACTION: Confirm with the Long Service Corporation (longservice.nsw.gov.au) whether your sub arrangements trigger any obligations. If your subs are sole traders with multiple clients and their own tools, they are unlikely to be covered. But get confirmation in writing.

If it does apply: you must register as a "building employer" and pay a levy (currently ~0.35% of wages/payments).
