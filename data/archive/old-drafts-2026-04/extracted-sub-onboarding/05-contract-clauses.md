**R6 C2**: 1

**R6 C3**: INDEPENDENT
CONTRACTOR STATUS

**R6 C4**: The Subcontractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, or partner of the Principal. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship. The Subcontractor is responsible for their own tax, superannuation (if applicable), insurance, and business expenses.

**R6 C5**: SHAM CONTRACTING PROTECTION: First line of defence. But the clause alone isn't enough — the real working relationship must match. If it walks like an employee, it's an employee regardless of what the contract says.

**R7 C2**: 2

**R7 C3**: SCOPE OF WORK

**R7 C4**: The Subcontractor will perform bathroom regrouting and/or resurfacing services as dispatched by the Principal via ServiceM8 or other agreed method. The Subcontractor is not required to accept every job offered and may decline without penalty. The Subcontractor may delegate or subcontract work to a qualified replacement with prior written approval of the Principal.

**R7 C5**: SHAM PROTECTION: "Not required to accept every job" + delegation right are critical. If they MUST accept AND can't delegate, they look like an employee. The delegation right must genuinely exist — not be a sham.

**R8 C2**: 3

**R8 C3**: PAYMENT TERMS

**R8 C4**: The Principal will pay the Subcontractor a flat rate per job as set out in the Rate Schedule (Clause 15). Payment within 7 business days of approved Tax Invoice + NSW Subcontractor's Statement. Payment is NOT conditional on whether the customer has paid the Principal (SOPA compliance). Weekly batch payments every Friday.

**R8 C5**: Flat rate per job (not hourly) supports contractor status. SOPA: you MUST pay subs for completed work even if customer hasn't paid you. This is the law under the Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 1999 (NSW).

**R9 C2**: 4

**R9 C3**: MATERIALS AND
EQUIPMENT

**R9 C4**: The Subcontractor will supply all materials, tools, and equipment necessary to perform the Services, at their own cost. Materials must be from the Approved Materials list provided by the Principal. Non-approved materials = sub bears full cost of rectification. Resurface: Hawk Glass-Tech only (via ARS). Regrout: Davco Sanitised (preferred), Ardex FG8, Laticrete PermaColor. Epoxy: Ardex EG15, Davco K10, Laticrete SpectraLOCK.

**R9 C5**: SHAM PROTECTION: Sub supplying own tools + materials is a key indicator of genuine contractor relationship. Approved materials list protects quality — if sub uses White Knight or Dulux (consumer-grade), finish fails in months and you're liable under consumer guarantee.

**R10 C2**: 5

**R10 C3**: QUALITY STANDARDS
AND WARRANTY

**R10 C4**: The Subcontractor warrants that all work will be performed:
(a) In a professional and workmanlike manner
(b) In accordance with the Principal's quality standards
(c) Using only approved materials
(d) With a 12-month warranty on workmanship and materials

Warranty covers defects in workmanship and materials, not customer misuse, normal wear, or pre-existing structural/waterproofing defects.

**R10 C5**: THE MOST IMPORTANT CLAUSE. Without this: sub does bad work, walks away, you refund the customer $1,660 and have zero recourse. With this: sub must fix it at their own cost within the warranty period.

**R11 C2**: 6

**R11 C3**: RECTIFICATION
AND DEDUCTIONS

**R11 C4**: If the Subcontractor fails to rectify a defect within 5 business days of notification, the Principal may engage another sub to rectify. The cost is deducted from any outstanding payments OR invoiced to the original sub if no payments are outstanding.

**R11 C5**: YOUR FINANCIAL PROTECTION: Without this, if a sub refuses to fix their work, you pay twice — once to the original sub and again to the replacement. This clause lets you deduct the cost.

**R12 C2**: 7

**R12 C3**: PHOTO DOCUMENTATION

**R12 C4**: The Subcontractor must take and submit photographs of every job:
(a) Before work begins (full area + close-ups)
(b) During work (progress shots)
(c) After completion (same angles as before)

Photos submitted same day via ServiceM8 or agreed channel. Payment withheld until photos received.

**R12 C5**: QUALITY CONTROL: Photos are your eyes on every job. Without them you're blind. Also serves as evidence if customer disputes quality, and feeds social proof (before/after) for marketing.

**R13 C2**: 8

**R13 C3**: INSURANCE
REQUIREMENTS

**R13 C4**: The Subcontractor must maintain at all times:
(a) Public Liability Insurance — minimum $10M
(b) Personal Accident / Income Protection insurance — RECOMMENDED
(c) Workers Compensation — MANDATORY if sub has any employees

Sub must provide Certificate of Currency before first job and upon each renewal. Sub must notify Principal immediately if insurance lapses.

**R13 C5**: YOUR #1 FINANCIAL RISK: If a sub injures themselves or damages property and has no insurance, YOU'RE exposed. $10M PL is industry standard. Personal accident protects the sub (sole traders get no workers comp for themselves).

**R14 C2**: 9

**R14 C3**: NON-SOLICITATION

**R14 C4**: The Subcontractor must not directly solicit or perform work for any customer introduced by the Principal for 12 months after the sub's last job for that specific customer. Applies ONLY to customers the sub was directly dispatched to service (not your entire customer base). Sub must not hand out business cards, flyers, or personal contact details to customers.

**R14 C5**: BUSINESS PROTECTION: Without this, sub meets your customer, hands them a card, offers 20% less next time. You paid for the lead, they steal the relationship. Narrowed to personally-serviced customers only for UCT compliance.

**R15 C2**: 10

**R15 C3**: CONFIDENTIALITY
AND PRICING
PROHIBITION

**R15 C4**: The Subcontractor must keep confidential all customer details, pricing, business methods, and operational information. Survives termination.

ADDITIONALLY: The Subcontractor must not discuss, enquire about, or attempt to discover the pricing charged by the Principal to customers. If a customer asks the sub about pricing, the sub must respond: "All pricing goes through the office." Breach of pricing prohibition = written warning; second offence = termination.

**R15 C5**: Prevents subs sharing your customer database, pricing structure, or operational details with competitors. The pricing enquiry prohibition is critical — a sub doing a $450 job who discovers the customer paid $1,660 may demand more money, poach the customer, or undermine your brand.

**R16 C2**: 11

**R16 C3**: NSW SUBCONTRACTOR'S
STATEMENT

**R16 C4**: The Subcontractor must provide a completed NSW Subcontractor's Statement with each payment claim / Tax Invoice. Statutory form declaring all wages, super, workers comp, and payroll tax obligations are up to date.

**R16 C5**: NSW LAW: Under s175B Workers Compensation Act 1987, if you don't collect this statement, you become personally liable for the sub's unpaid workers comp premiums. Collect it with EVERY payment — not just the first one.

**R17 C2**: 12

**R17 C3**: TERMINATION AND
BREACH SEVERITY
TIERS

**R17 C4**: Either party may terminate this Agreement:
(a) With 14 days written notice without cause
(b) Immediately for material breach as defined below

All outstanding payments for completed work must be paid within 7 business days of termination (SOPA compliance).

IMMEDIATE TERMINATION (no warning):
• Insurance lapsed + worked uninsured (Clause 8)
• WHS violation creating imminent danger (Clause 14)
• Disturbing suspected asbestos (Clause 19)
• Attending site under influence (Clause 26c)
• Entering restricted areas of customer's home (Clause 26e)
• Fraud, theft, dishonesty

WARNING FIRST, THEN TERMINATE IF REPEATED:
• Unapproved materials (Clause 4)
• Defective work + refusing to rectify (Clauses 5+6)
• Repeated failure to submit photos (Clause 7)
• Soliciting introduced customers (Clause 9)
• Pricing discussion or confidentiality breach (Clause 10)
• Posting customer photos on own social media (Clause 20)
• Unapproved extra work on site (Clause 21)
• Misusing customer data (Clause 23)
• Repeated lateness, poor cleanup, smoking, direct selling, unapproved helpers (Clauses 26, 27)
• Failure to return on multi-day job without notice (Clause 31)
• Cancelling committed job with <48hrs notice (Clause 32)
• Failure to respond to warranty callback within 48hrs (Clause 29)
• 3+ late cancellations in 90 days (Clause 32) = escalated to immediate termination

NOT GROUNDS FOR TERMINATION (procedural/payment clauses):
Clauses 1, 2, 3, 11, 13, 15, 16, 17, 22, 24, 25, 28, 30

**R17 C5**: Without defined severity tiers, "material breach" is ambiguous — a sub could argue that using the wrong grout brand isn't material. These tiers make it clear what happens for each type of breach. Mutual termination + payment on termination required for UCT compliance.

**R18 C2**: 13

**R18 C3**: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**R18 C4**: Any dispute will be resolved by:
(a) Direct negotiation between the parties (14 days)
(b) Mediation via NSW Small Business Commissioner (free service)
(c) If unresolved, either party may pursue legal remedies

Governing law: NSW.

**R18 C5**: Mediation first = cheaper than court. NSW Small Business Commissioner offers free/low-cost mediation for disputes under $20K.

**R19 C2**: 14

**R19 C3**: WHS OBLIGATIONS

**R19 C4**: The Subcontractor is responsible for ensuring workplace health and safety at all times:
(a) Wear appropriate PPE (especially respirator for resurfacing)
(b) Ensure adequate ventilation during spray/chemical application
(c) Report any safety incidents to Principal within 2 hours
(d) Complete asbestos awareness training (SafeWork NSW free online course)
(e) Follow all SafeWork NSW guidelines for the relevant work

**R19 C5**: WHS Act 2011: You have a duty of care as the PCBU. Sub also has WHS duties. This clause documents the shared responsibility and ensures the sub maintains safe practices on every site.

**R20 C2**: 15

**R20 C3**: RATE SCHEDULE
(attachment)

**R20 C4**: Attach a separate Rate Schedule listing:
• Flat rate per job type (regrout small/medium/large, resurface, silicone only, etc.)
• Travel allowance per zone

Principal must give at least 14 days' written notice of any changes to the Rate Schedule. Changes apply only to future jobs, not jobs already accepted. Sub may decline future work if they disagree with rate changes — no penalty.

**R20 C5**: Keep rates in an attachment, not the main agreement body. This way you can update rates without re-executing the entire contract. 14-day notice + right to decline = UCT compliant.

**R22 C2**: 17

**R22 C3**: TAX REPORTING

**R23 C2**: 18

**R23 C3**: STRATA PROPERTY
CLAUSE

**R23 C4**: For work at strata-titled properties, the Subcontractor must not commence work until the Principal confirms:
(a) Strata approval obtained (if required)
(b) Work hours confirmed (typically 8am-5pm weekdays, no weekends)
(c) Access arrangements confirmed with building manager

**R23 C5**: STRATA PROTECTION: Strata properties have by-laws about noise hours, tradesperson access, and common area protection. Breaching them = fines for the owner + complaints to your business.

**R24 C2**: 19

**R24 C3**: ASBESTOS CLAUSE

**R24 C4**: The Subcontractor must not commence or continue work where asbestos-containing materials are suspected. If the Subcontractor suspects asbestos (pre-1990 tiles, adhesive, or backing), they must:
(a) STOP work immediately
(b) Do NOT disturb the material
(c) Notify the Principal immediately
(d) Leave the site if necessary for safety

Disturbance of suspected asbestos = immediate termination.

**R24 C5**: NSW WHS COMPLIANCE: SafeWork NSW requires all workers to stop work if asbestos is suspected. Penalties for unlicensed asbestos removal: up to $50,000 for individuals. This clause makes your position absolute.

**R25 C2**: 20

**R25 C3**: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
AND PHOTO LICENCE

**R25 C4**: All photographs, videos, and visual recordings taken by the Subcontractor during the course of work are owned by the Principal. The Sub grants the Principal a perpetual, royalty-free licence to use all job photos for marketing, social media, proposals, and training. The Sub must NOT post customer photos on their own social media or use them for marketing without written permission. Survives termination.

**R25 C5**: MARKETING PROTECTION: Without this, a sub could demand you stop using job photos in your marketing. Also prevents subs posting customer bathroom photos on their own social media — privacy concern.

**R26 C2**: 21

**R26 C3**: VARIATION / CHANGE
ORDER PROCESS

**R26 C4**: If the Subcontractor identifies during work that additional or different work is needed, they must:
(a) STOP work on the additional scope
(b) Notify the Principal within 1 hour with photo + text description
(c) Wait for written approval before proceeding

No verbal approvals. Unapproved additional work is at the sub's own cost. The Principal will requote the customer and confirm the adjusted scope and rate.

**R26 C5**: FINANCIAL PROTECTION: Without this, subs can inflate jobs on site. Customer gets a surprise bill, blames you, demands refund. With this clause, all scope changes go through you.

Sub must not retain customer data after job completion, share it with third parties, or use it for any purpose other than the assigned work. Compliant with Privacy Act 1988. Survives termination.

**R29 C2**: 24

**R29 C3**: FORCE MAJEURE

**R29 C4**: Neither party will be liable for failure to perform obligations under this Agreement caused by events beyond reasonable control, including:
(a) Extreme heat (>39°C — spray painting is unsafe)
(b) Bushfire smoke (respiratory hazard for coatings work)
(c) Floods, storms, or severe weather
(d) Government restrictions or lockdowns

Affected party must notify the other within 24 hours.

**R29 C5**: PRACTICAL PROTECTION: Sydney gets extreme heat days. Spray painting above 39°C = product failure + safety risk. This clause protects both parties from penalty for weather-related delays.

**R30 C2**: 25

**R30 C3**: MUTUAL INDEMNITY

**R30 C4**: Each party indemnifies the other against all loss, damage, cost, and expense (including legal fees) arising from:
(a) SUB indemnifies PRINCIPAL for: defective work, property damage caused by sub, personal injury caused by sub's negligence, breach of WHS obligations
(b) PRINCIPAL indemnifies SUB for: incorrect job specifications, dispatching sub to unsafe site, failure to pay within agreed terms

Survives termination.

**R30 C5**: UCT COMPLIANCE: A one-sided indemnity (only sub indemnifies you) could be challenged as unfair. Mutual indemnity is balanced and protects both parties.

**R31 C2**: 26

**R31 C3**: PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT
AND ON-SITE
BEHAVIOUR

**R31 C4**: The Subcontractor must at all times when performing Services:

(a) PRESENTATION: Arrive in clean work attire with a clean, organised vehicle
(b) PUNCTUALITY: Arrive within scheduled window. Notify Principal and customer if running >15 minutes late
(c) SUBSTANCES: Zero tolerance for alcohol, drugs, or any impairing substance on site. Immediate termination.
(d) SMOKING: No smoking on customer's property at any time
(e) RESTRICTED AREAS: Remain in the work area only. Do not enter other rooms, use customer's belongings, or access their wifi
(f) CLEANUP: Leave the bathroom and surrounding area cleaner than found. Remove all waste, packaging, and protective sheeting
(g) PRICING: Do not discuss pricing with customers. "All pricing goes through the office."
(h) NO DIRECT SELLING: Do not offer additional services, hand out business cards, or upsell directly
(i) UNAPPROVED PERSONS: Do not bring unapproved people to site (see Clause 27)
(j) RESPECT: Treat customer's property with care. Do not move belongings without permission.

**R31 C5**: BRAND PROTECTION: Your sub is the only person the customer ever meets. One bad experience = 1-star review + refund request. This clause sets clear behavioural expectations. Walk through in plain language during onboarding briefing.

**R32 C2**: 27

**R32 C3**: UNAPPROVED PERSONS
ON SITE

**R32 C4**: The Subcontractor must not bring any employee, assistant, helper, or other person to a customer's property without 48 hours written notice to the Principal, including the person's ABN and PL insurance certificate. Principal must approve in writing before any additional person attends site. Unapproved person on site = sub bears full liability for any damage/injury + breach of contract.

**R32 C5**: SAFETY + INSURANCE: If an unapproved, uninsured person shows up at a customer's home and gets injured or damages property, you have zero coverage. This clause prevents it and makes the sub liable.

**R33 C2**: 28

**R33 C3**: CANCELLATION AND
CALLOUT FEE

**R33 C4**: If the Principal cancels or customer cancels a dispatched job LESS than 24 hours before the scheduled start time, sub receives a $100 (ex GST) callout fee.

If sub arrives at site and cannot access the property (customer not home, strata access denied, locked gate) through no fault of the sub, the same $100 callout fee applies. Sub must wait 15 minutes and attempt phone contact before leaving, with a time-stamped photo of the property.

Callout fee does NOT apply if: (a) sub was late and customer left, (b) sub cancelled, (c) job cancelled with 24+ hours notice.

Callout fee invoiced in the normal payment cycle.

**R33 C5**: PROTECTS sub from lost income on wasted trips. Without this, subs leave after their first no-access. $100 covers fuel + lost opportunity. Offset by customer's non-refundable deposit ($80-$200).

**R34 C2**: 29

**R34 C3**: WARRANTY CALLBACK
OBLIGATIONS

**R34 C4**: Sub must rectify warranty claims within the 12-month warranty period (Clause 5) at their own cost, including travel and materials. Response within 48 hours, rectification within 7 business days.

If sub fails to respond/complete: Principal may engage another sub and deduct cost from outstanding payments or invoice the original sub.

WARRANTY SURVIVES TERMINATION: Obligation continues for 12 months after each job, even if sub has left your network.

Sub NOT liable for: customer misuse, normal wear, pre-existing structural/waterproofing defects.

**R34 C5**: Without "survives termination," a sub could do a bad job, quit the next day, and you pay another sub to fix it. 48hr response and 7-day rectification give the sub fair time while protecting the customer. No holdback — enforced via deduction right.

**R35 C2**: 30

**R35 C3**: PHOTO-TO-REALITY
MISMATCH
(scope adjustment)

**R35 C4**: Customer quotes are based on photos. If the sub arrives and actual scope differs materially from photos:

1. STOP work on the additional/different scope
2. Contact Principal within 30 minutes with photos + description
3. Principal requotes the customer
4. Sub proceeds ONLY after Principal provides written confirmation of adjusted scope and rate

Sub is paid based on ACTUAL confirmed scope, not the original photo estimate. Sub must NEVER quote the customer directly.

**R35 C5**: Happens in ~15-20% of photo-based quotes. Without this clause, either the sub eats the loss (they leave), you eat it (margin dies), or the customer gets surprised (trust dies). This creates a clean requote process.

**R36 C2**: 31

**R36 C3**: MULTI-DAY JOB
CONTINUITY

**R36 C4**: For jobs requiring 2+ consecutive days (full bathroom packages, large resurface with curing):

Sub commits to completing all scheduled days consecutively unless force majeure (Clause 24) or written agreement. If sub cannot return, notify Principal by 6pm the night before.

Failure to return without notice = WARNING. Second occurrence = termination. Payment: single flat rate for complete job, not per-day. Sub must leave work area safe and secure between days.

**R36 C5**: A bath resurface takes 2 days. If sub doesn't return day 2, customer has unusable bathroom with half-cured coating. Single flat rate prevents subs doing day 1 and claiming half pay.

**R37 C2**: 32

**R37 C3**: JOB DISPATCH
ACCEPTANCE AND
COMMITMENT

**R37 C4**: Sub may accept or decline any offered job without penalty. Once the sub ACCEPTS a job, it is committed — the customer has been notified and is expecting the sub.

Cancelling committed job: 48+ hours notice required. <48hrs notice = WARNING. <24hrs notice = $100 fee (deducted from next payment). 3+ late cancellations in 90 days = termination.

Declining a job ≠ cancelling a committed job. Declining is always free.

**R37 C5**: Closes the loop between customer "You're booked for Tuesday 2pm" SMS and the sub contract. Without this, subs accept then cancel freely, leaving you scrambling. The $100 mirrors the callout fee — symmetry = fairness.
