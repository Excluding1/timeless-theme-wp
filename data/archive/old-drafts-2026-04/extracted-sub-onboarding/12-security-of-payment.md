**R6 C2**: Does SOPA apply
to your business?

**R6 C3**: YES. Regrouting and resurfacing = "maintenance" of buildings. The Act covers "construction, alteration, repair, restoration, maintenance" of any works forming part of land.

Your subs perform construction work under a construction contract (your subcontractor agreement) in NSW.

**R6 C4**: Your sub agreement must be compatible with SOPA. Any clause that tries to exclude SOPA rights is VOID — the Act overrides contract terms on payment.

**R7 C2**: Sub's right to
progress payments

**R7 C3**: Your subs have a statutory right to claim progress payments for work completed, even if your contract doesn't expressly allow it.

Payments to subcontractors are due within 20 business days of a payment claim being served (unless your contract specifies a shorter period).

**R7 C4**: Your contract says 7-14 days. That's fine — it's shorter than the 20-day statutory maximum. Just make sure you actually pay within that period.

**R8 C2**: Payment claims
under SOPA

**R8 C3**: Your sub can serve a formal "payment claim" on you. A valid payment claim must:
• Identify the construction work done
• State the amount claimed
• State it's made under the Act

Once you receive a valid payment claim, you have 10 business days to respond with a "payment schedule" (stating what you'll pay and why, if less than claimed).

**R8 C4**: If you DON'T respond within 10 business days:
• You become liable for the FULL amount claimed
• The sub can apply for adjudication
• You lose the right to raise defences

NEVER ignore a SOPA payment claim. Always respond within 10 business days.

**R9 C2**: Adjudication

**R9 C3**: If there's a dispute, the sub can apply for adjudication through an Authorised Nominating Authority. An adjudicator decides the dispute (usually within 10-15 business days).

Adjudication is fast and relatively cheap ($500-$3,000 depending on amount). It's designed to keep money flowing, not to determine final rights.

**R9 C4**: Adjudication decisions are enforceable like a court judgment. You must pay the adjudicated amount.

You can still dispute the merits later in court, but you pay the adjudicated amount in the meantime.

Prevention: pay your subs on time. 95% of SOPA claims arise from late payment, not quality disputes.

**R10 C2**: Supporting statement
(head contractor
obligation)

**R10 C3**: When you serve a payment claim on your customer (the property owner), you must include a "supporting statement" declaring that all subcontractors have been paid for the work.

This applies when you're the head contractor and you have subs working on the project.

**R10 C4**: Failure to include a supporting statement: penalty up to $22,000.

In practice: for most of your small residential jobs, you're billing the customer a single amount (not progress claims). But for larger commercial or PM work, this may apply.

**R11 C2**: How to make SOPA
work FOR you
(not against you)

**R11 C3**: 1. Pay subs on time — within the 7-14 days stated in your contract. This prevents 99% of SOPA issues.

2. If withholding payment for quality reasons, issue a payment schedule within 10 business days explaining exactly why and how much you're withholding.

3. Use your rectification clause (Contract Clause 6) — deduct the cost of fixing bad work, but document everything with photos and written notice.

4. Keep records of every invoice, payment, and communication. SOPA disputes are won on documentation.

**R11 C4**: SOPA is actually a GOOD thing for your business model. It forces professionalism on both sides:

• You pay subs promptly = subs stay loyal and do good work
• Subs who invoice properly = clean records for TPAR
• Disputes resolved quickly = less disruption to your business

The businesses that fear SOPA are the ones who pay late. Don't be that business.

**R12 C2**: SOPA clause for
your sub agreement

**R12 C3**: Add to your contract:

"The parties acknowledge that the Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 1999 (NSW) applies to this Agreement. Payment claims must be submitted monthly (or per job completion). The Principal will respond to any payment claim within 10 business days. Payment is due within [14] business days of a valid payment claim."

**R12 C4**: This clause:
• Acknowledges SOPA (shows good faith)
• Sets a payment claim cycle (per job or monthly)
• Confirms your response timeframe (10 days)
• Sets payment terms within the statutory maximum

Add this as Clause 16 in your Sub Agreement.
