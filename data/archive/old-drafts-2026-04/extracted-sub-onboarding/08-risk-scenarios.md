**R2 C2**: RISK SCENARIOS — 28 SCENARIOS WITH PROTECTIONS

**R3 C2**: Every scenario below WILL happen. The question is whether you're protected when it does. Each scenario maps to specific contract clauses.

**R5 C3**: What Happens Without Protection

**R5 C4**: Your Protection

**R6 C2**: 1. Sub does bad work
(peeling, cracking,
rough finish)

**R6 C3**: Customer demands full refund ($1,660). Sub keeps their fee ($600). You're out $2,260.

**R6 C4**: Clauses 5 (warranty) + 6 (rectification/deduction). Sub must fix at own cost within 5 days. If they refuse, you hire another sub and deduct the cost.

**R7 C2**: 2. Sub no-shows
on the day

**R7 C3**: Customer waited all day. Furious. 1-star Google review. You scramble to reschedule.

**R7 C4**: Clause 12: immediate termination for material breach. Clause 32: committed job cancellation = fee + warning. Post-deposit template: booking confirmation SMS sets customer expectation.

**R8 C2**: 3. Sub steals your
customer (direct work)

**R8 C3**: Sub gives customer their card. Customer calls sub directly next time. You lose the lead cost + lifetime value.

**R8 C4**: Clause 9 (non-solicitation, 12 months). Clause 26h (no direct selling/business cards). Enforceable because narrowed to personally-serviced customers only (UCT compliant).

**R9 C2**: 4. Sub injures
themselves on site

**R9 C3**: Sole trader with no insurance may claim against your PL or sue. Potential $100K+ claim.

**R9 C4**: Vetting #3 (PL insurance) + #4 (personal accident). Clause 8 (insurance mandatory). Clause 1 (independent contractor status). WHS obligations (Clause 14).

**R10 C2**: 5. Sub damages
customer's property
(overspray, chemical
spill, broken tile)

**R10 C3**: Customer demands compensation from YOU. Could be $500 (cleaning) to $10,000+ (tile replacement).

**R10 C4**: Sub's PL insurance covers this. Clause 8 (insurance mandatory). Clause 25 (sub indemnifies for damage). Clause 26f (cleanup standards).

**R11 C2**: 6. Customer claims
asbestos exposure

**R11 C3**: Pre-1990 property. Sub disturbed asbestos tiles. Potential $50K+ fine + health liability.

**R11 C4**: Quote form captures property age. Clause 19 (stop work, never disturb). Clause 14 (asbestos awareness). Rejection criteria for suspected asbestos properties.

**R12 C2**: 7. Sub classified as
"deemed worker"
→ workers comp liability

**R12 C3**: SIRA determines sub is employee. You owe workers comp premiums, back-pay, penalties.

**R12 C4**: Sham contracting protections (Sheet 06). Contract structure: flat rate, own tools/materials, delegation right, multiple clients, result-based.

**R13 C2**: 8. ATO determines
sham contracting
→ back-pay super + tax

**R13 C3**: PAYG withholding penalties + Super Guarantee Charge. Can go back several years.

**R13 C4**: Contract structure + real working arrangement must match. All 9 indicators on Sheet 06 addressed. ATO Super Eligibility Tool screenshot + accountant confirmation.

**R14 C2**: 9. Sub uses cheap
materials (finish
fails in 3-6 months)

**R14 C3**: Customer complains. You warranty it. Send another sub to fix. Cost: $400-$800.

**R14 C4**: Clause 4 (approved materials specified). Clause 5 (warranty). Photo review catches wrong products. Vetting question #12 (what products do you use?).

**R15 C2**: 10. Customer refuses
to pay balance

**R15 C3**: You've already paid the sub. Customer won't pay remaining $1,200+.

**R15 C4**: Photo documentation (Clause 7) proves work quality. 10% deposit already collected. Balance due SMS with payment link. Stripe payment = easy. Small claims court if needed.

**R16 C2**: 11. Sub uses wrong
materials despite
contract (e.g. White
Knight instead of
Hawk Glass-Tech)

**R16 C3**: Resurface starts peeling in 3 months. You're liable under consumer guarantee. Full redo costs $800-$1,200.

**R16 C4**: Clause 4 (approved materials). Clause 5 (warranty covers materials). Photo review: check product in before/during photos. Clause 12: unapproved materials = warning then termination.

**R17 C2**: 12. Sub poaches
customer directly
(business card, cheaper
price offer)

**R17 C3**: You lose customer AND future referrals. Sub builds competing business using your leads.

**R17 C4**: Clause 9 (non-solicitation). Clause 10 (confidentiality). Clause 26g+h (no pricing discussion, no direct selling). Terminate + enforce non-solicitation.

**R18 C2**: 13. Sub posts customer
photos on their own
social media

**R18 C3**: Customer privacy breach. Could be liable under Privacy Act. Customer complains.

**R18 C4**: Clause 20 (IP/photo licence). Clause 23 (data/privacy). Written warning → termination. Onboarding briefing covers this explicitly.

**R19 C2**: 14. Sub damages property
beyond work area
(scratched tiles,
cracked tap, stained
carpet)

**R19 C3**: Customer demands compensation. If sub's insurance lapsed, you're exposed.

**R19 C4**: Clause 8 (insurance $10M PL). Clause 25 (mutual indemnity). Clause 26e (restricted areas). Insurance expiry tracking (Sheet 07, system #8).

**R20 C2**: 15. Sub gets injured
and claims employment
status (workers comp)

**R20 C3**: If classified as employee: workers comp, back-pay, super, payroll tax. Potentially $50K+.

**R20 C4**: Clause 1 (contractor status). Clause 8 (personal accident insurance recommended). Sham contracting protections (Sheet 06). Contract with Pty Ltd subs = bulletproof.

**R21 C2**: 16. Unfair contract term
challenge from sub
or sub's lawyer

**R21 C3**: Clause declared void. Penalty up to $50M per contravention (realistically: clause struck out).

**R21 C4**: Clause 22 (UCT compliance review by Sprintlaw). Most likely targets: Clause 6 (deductions), 9 (non-solicitation), 12 (termination). All already narrowed for UCT compliance.

**R22 C2**: 17. Sub works for
competitor and takes
your systems/pricing
/customer list

**R22 C3**: Competitor undercuts using your rate card. Contacts your customers directly.

**R22 C4**: Clause 10 (confidentiality — survives termination). Clause 9 (non-solicitation). Don't share full customer database with subs — only job-specific details.

**R23 C2**: 18. Sub asks customer
how much they're
paying (discovers
your margin)

**R23 C3**: Sub calculates your 50-70% markup. Gets resentful. Demands higher rates or poaches directly.

**R23 C4**: Clause 10 (pricing prohibition). Clause 26g ("all pricing goes through the office"). Written warning → termination. Onboarding briefing: scripted response for subs.

**R24 C2**: 19. Sub arrives late
(1-2 hours, not a
full no-show)

**R24 C3**: Customer took time off work. Annoyed. If repeated, they won't rebook or refer.

**R24 C4**: Clause 26b (notify if >15 min delayed). Process: verbal warning → written → formal → removal. Day-before reminder SMS reduces this.

**R25 C2**: 20. Sub arrives under
the influence or
unprofessional

**R25 C3**: Customer refuses entry. Or sub does bad work while impaired. Safety risk.

**R25 C4**: Clause 26c (zero tolerance). Clause 14 (WHS). Process: stand down immediately, don't accuse, reschedule customer, formal meeting within 24hrs. Confirmed = immediate termination.

**R26 C2**: 21. Customer feels
unsafe with sub
in their home

**R26 C3**: Customer uncomfortable but doesn't speak up. Leaves 1-star review. Never rebooks.

**R26 C4**: Clause 26 (professional conduct). Process: take seriously, call sub to wrap up and leave, reschedule with different sub, investigate after.

**R27 C2**: 22. Customer reports
missing belongings

**R27 C3**: Whether true or not, reputational emergency. Customer blames your business.

**R27 C4**: Clause 26e (restricted areas). Clause 25 (indemnity). Process: document, contact sub, advise customer of right to file police report. Never dispatch that sub to same customer again.

**R28 C2**: 23. Sub brings
unapproved person
to job site

**R28 C3**: Unapproved person has no insurance, no vetting, no ABN. If they get injured, you're exposed.

**R28 C4**: Clause 27 (unapproved persons). Process: instruct removal, formal written warning. If damage caused, sub fully liable.

**R29 C2**: 24. Customer cancels
morning of the job
(sub already dispatched)

**R29 C3**: Sub loses half a day + fuel + prepped materials. Without protection, subs leave your network.

**R29 C4**: Clause 28 (callout fee): Sub receives $100. Offset by customer's non-refundable deposit. If >10% cancellation rate, review booking confirmation process.

**R30 C2**: 25. Sub arrives but
customer not home /
access denied

**R30 C3**: Sub drove to site, can't get in. Losing money standing there.

**R30 C4**: Clause 28 (callout fee): 15-min wait, phone contact attempt, time-stamped photo, then leave. $100 callout fee. Prevention: day-before reminder SMS + strata confirmation.

**R31 C2**: 26. Photos under-
represented the job
(actual scope bigger
than quoted)

**R31 C3**: You quoted $1,200 from photos. Actually a $1,800 job. Sub either eats it (unfair) or customer gets surprised (trust broken).

**R31 C4**: Clause 30 (photo-to-reality mismatch): Sub stops, photos discrepancy, calls you. You requote customer. Sub paid on actual scope. Never quote customer directly.

**R32 C2**: 27. Sub doesn't return
for day 2 of
multi-day job

**R32 C3**: Customer has unusable bathroom with half-cured coating. Massive complaint. Finding a replacement sub to finish someone else's work is rare.

**R32 C4**: Clause 31 (multi-day continuity): Committed to consecutive days. No return without notice = warning. No payment until all days complete. Confirm with sub at end of day 1.

**R33 C2**: 28. Sub accepts job,
customer gets booking
confirmation, then
sub cancels last minute

**R33 C3**: Customer received "You're booked for Tuesday 2pm." You have to call and reschedule. Trust damage.

**R33 C4**: Clause 32 (dispatch commitment): <48hrs = warning, <24hrs = $100 fee, 3+ late cancellations = termination. Maintain backup sub bench. Never tell customer "the sub cancelled" — say "we need to reschedule."
