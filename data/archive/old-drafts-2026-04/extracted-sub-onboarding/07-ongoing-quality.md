**R4 C2**: System

**R4 C3**: How It Works

**R5 C2**: 1. CUSTOMER NPS
(every job)

**R5 C3**: Every customer gets NPS SMS 2 hours after job complete (already built in Sheet 13 of Quote Output Templates).

Track NPS per sub. Monthly review:
• Average NPS below 7 → performance conversation
• Average NPS below 5 → stand down pending review
• 2+ scores of 1-3 in any month → immediate stand down

**R6 C2**: 2. PHOTO REVIEW
(every job)

**R6 C3**: Sub submits before/during/after photos on every job. Team reviews the AFTER photos within 24 hours.

Use the Quality Rubric (Sheet 04) to spot-score at least 20% of jobs per sub per month. You don't need to inspect every job in person — photos catch 80% of quality issues.

**R7 C2**: 3. RANDOM SITE
INSPECTION
(monthly)

**R7 C3**: Visit 1-2 completed jobs per sub per month unannounced (within 24 hours of completion, before the customer starts using it heavily).

Score using the Quality Rubric. Compare with sub's photo documentation — are photos accurate or hiding problems?

Budget 2-3 hours per month for inspections. This is your single highest-ROI activity.

**R8 C2**: 4. CALLBACK RATE
TRACKING

**R8 C3**: Track warranty callbacks per sub:
• Acceptable: <5% callback rate (1 in 20 jobs)
• Warning: 5-10% callback rate
• Unacceptable: >10% callback rate → stand down

Callback causes: track whether it's workmanship (sub's fault) or materials (product issue) or customer misuse (not a quality issue).

**R9 C2**: 5. QUARTERLY
PERFORMANCE REVIEW

**R9 C3**: Meet (phone or video) with each active sub every quarter.

Cover:
• NPS scores and trends
• Callback rate
• Photo documentation compliance
• Any customer feedback themes
• Rate review (if applicable)
• Their feedback on your dispatch, communication, job briefs

This isn't a boss-employee meeting — it's a business-to-business review. Good subs appreciate feedback and want to improve.

**R10 C2**: 6. SUB REMOVAL
PROCESS

**R10 C3**: STAGE 1 — VERBAL WARNING: Specific feedback on the issue. Document it (email follow-up confirming the conversation).

STAGE 2 — WRITTEN WARNING: Formal letter/email citing specific quality failures with dates and evidence (photos, NPS scores). "If not improved within 30 days, we will terminate the agreement."

STAGE 3 — TERMINATION: 14 days written notice per the Agreement (or immediate if material breach like insurance lapse, no-show, customer safety issue). Pay all outstanding invoices. Professional exit.

EXCEPTION — IMMEDIATE TERMINATION: Serious safety breach, theft, fraud, assault, insurance lapse, or customer property damage through negligence. No warning stages needed. Terminate immediately per Clause 12(b) of Agreement.

**R11 C2**: 7. SUB SCORECARD
(track in spreadsheet)

**R11 C3**: Maintain a simple spreadsheet per sub:

Columns: Sub Name | Month | Jobs Completed | Avg NPS | Callback Count | Callback Rate | Photo Compliance % | Random Inspection Score | Notes

Review monthly. Share with the sub quarterly (transparency builds trust and accountability).

**R13 C2**: 8. INSURANCE EXPIRY
TRACKING
(critical)

**R13 C3**: Track PL insurance and personal accident insurance expiry dates for every sub in a spreadsheet or Xero.

Set calendar reminders 30 DAYS before each policy expires.

Process:
• 30 days before expiry → SMS/email sub: "Your PL insurance expires on [date]. Please send updated Certificate of Currency by [date -7 days]."
• 7 days before → follow-up if not received
• On expiry day → if no updated certificate → STAND DOWN sub immediately. No jobs dispatched until certificate received.

NEVER dispatch a sub with expired insurance. If they cause damage or injury while uninsured, YOU are liable.

Check annually: Is their coverage still $10M minimum? Has the insured name changed? Does the policy cover the type of work you dispatch?

**R14 C2**: 9. OFFBOARDING
PROCESS
(when sub leaves)

**R14 C3**: When a sub exits (voluntary or termination):

1. Final payment: Pay all outstanding invoices within 7 business days (SOPA compliance)
2. Collect final NSW Subcontractor's Statement
3. Confirm return/deletion of any confidential materials (customer data, rate schedule)
4. Remove from ServiceM8 + Slack
5. Update Xero (mark as inactive, don't delete — need records for TPAR)
6. Record reason for departure in sub file
7. If terminated for cause → document the breach, evidence, and process followed
8. Non-solicitation period starts from last day of engagement (12 months)
9. Reassign any upcoming jobs to another sub + notify affected customers
10. Keep all records for 5-7 years (tax, TPAR, potential disputes)
