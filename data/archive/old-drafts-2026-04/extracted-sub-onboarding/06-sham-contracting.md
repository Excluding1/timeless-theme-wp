**R2 C2**: SHAM CONTRACTING — HOW TO STAY SAFE

**R3 C2**: Penalties: Up to $99,000 per contravention for small businesses (<15 employees). Up to $495,000 for businesses with 15+ employees. Plus back-pay of super, leave, workers comp. The ATO and Fair Work are actively targeting building + construction in 2025-2026.

**R5 C2**: Indicator

**R5 C3**: Looks Like a CONTRACTOR ✅

**R5 C4**: Looks Like an EMPLOYEE ❌

**R6 C2**: Control over HOW
work is done

**R6 C3**: Sub decides how to regrout/resurface. You set the quality standard and scope — they choose methods, tools, sequence.

**R6 C4**: You tell them exactly how to do each step. You provide a detailed procedure they must follow. You supervise them on site.

**R7 C2**: Tools and materials

**R7 C3**: Sub supplies their own tools, vehicle, and materials at their own cost.

**R7 C4**: You supply tools, materials, or vehicle. You provide a uniform or branded clothing.

**R8 C2**: Ability to refuse work

**R8 C3**: Sub can decline a job offer. No penalty for refusing.

**R8 C4**: Sub must accept every job dispatched. Penalised for refusing.

**R9 C2**: Works for others

**R9 C3**: Sub has other clients. Can and does work for competitors or take direct jobs.

**R9 C4**: Sub works exclusively for you. Not allowed to work for anyone else.

**R10 C2**: Financial risk

**R10 C3**: Sub quotes a flat rate. If job takes longer, they absorb the cost. If materials cost more, they absorb it.

**R10 C4**: You pay hourly. Sub has no financial risk — gets paid regardless of efficiency.

**R11 C2**: Delegation

**R11 C3**: Sub can send a qualified replacement to do the job if they're unavailable (with your approval of the replacement's quality).

**R11 C4**: The specific person must do the work. They cannot delegate or subcontract.

**R12 C2**: Integration into
your business

**R12 C3**: Sub is not part of your team meetings, doesn't have a company email, doesn't attend your office, doesn't wear your uniform.

**R12 C4**: Sub has a company email, attends team meetings, is listed on your website as "our team," wears your branded uniform.

**R13 C2**: Tax and super

**R13 C3**: Sub handles their own tax, BAS, ABN. Invoices you with a Tax Invoice. You don't withhold PAYG.

**R13 C4**: You withhold PAYG. You pay their super (note: super IS required if engaged "principally for labour" — see below).

**R14 C2**: Ongoing engagement
vs project-based

**R14 C3**: Engaged per job. No guarantee of ongoing work. Relationship can be paused or ended.

**R14 C4**: Guaranteed minimum hours per week. Ongoing, open-ended engagement that looks like permanent employment.

**R17 C2**: ⚠️ SUPERANNUATION NOTE

**R18 C2**: Under the ATO's 3-part test (s12(3) SGAA), you almost certainly do NOT owe super to your subs. All three must be true simultaneously: (1) contract mainly for labour, (2) paid for personal labour not a result, (3) cannot delegate. Your model fails all three: subs supply materials + tools + vehicle (not mainly labour), paid flat rate per completed job (result-based), and contract includes delegation right. If sub invoices through Pty Ltd = definitively no super. Run the ATO Super Guarantee Eligibility Tool + get accountant written confirmation ($100-$200). See Sheet 11 for full analysis.
