Request evidence of personal accident or income protection insurance. Not legally mandatory for you to require, but strongly recommended to protect yourself.

**R12 C4**: Statutory form. Sub declares they've paid all: wages, superannuation, workers comp premiums, and payroll tax for the work period.

⚠️ Under s175B Workers Compensation Act 1987: if you DON'T collect this statement, YOU become liable for their unpaid workers comp premiums.

Collect with EVERY payment, not just onboarding. Use the standard NSW form.

**R19 C5**: Red Flags

**R20 C2**: 11

**R20 C3**: How long have you been
doing regrout/resurface
work?

**R20 C4**: 2+ years full-time. Can describe specific jobs. Knows product names (Davco, Ardex, Hawk Glass-Tech). Has worked for a company or franchise before (Jim's, Surface Care, Bathroom Werx, etc).

**R20 C5**: Less than 1 year. Vague answers. Doesn't know product names. "I watched YouTube videos."

**R21 C2**: 12

**R21 C3**: What products/systems
do you use?

**R21 C4**: REGROUT: Names specific grout brands (Davco Sanitized, Ardex FG8, Laticrete PermaColor). Knows difference between cement and epoxy grout. Knows when to use each.
RESURFACE: Names professional coating systems (Hawk Glass-Tech, similar 2-part systems). Describes multi-coat process. Mentions primer + topcoat.

**R21 C5**: "Just whatever's at Bunnings." Names White Knight or Dulux Renovation Range (consumer-grade = peels within 1-2 years). Can't describe coating process.

**R22 C2**: 13

**R22 C3**: Walk me through your
process for a standard
shower regrout

**R22 C4**: Should describe: grout removal (multi-tool or rake), dust/debris cleanup, joint preparation, grout mixing, application with float, wiping excess, silicone removal, reapplication, cleanup, cure time advice to customer.

Key detail: mentions CURE TIME. If they don't mention it → they don't tell customers → customer uses shower too early → failure.

**R22 C5**: Skips steps. Doesn't mention silicone as separate from grout. Doesn't mention cure time. Says it takes "1 hour" for a full shower regrout (impossible — 3-5 hours minimum).

**R23 C2**: 14

**R23 C3**: Walk me through your
process for a bath
resurface

**R23 C4**: Should describe: surface assessment, repair chips/cracks, acid wash/degreasing, sanding, masking/protection of surrounding area, ventilation setup, primer coat (with dry time), topcoat (multiple coats with dry time between), final inspection, cleanup, cure time advice (48-72 hours).

Key detail: mentions MASKING. Poor masking = overspray damage = customer complaint = your problem.

**R23 C5**: Skips preparation steps. Doesn't mention masking. Says "done in 2 hours" (impossible for proper multi-coat — 4-8 hours minimum). Doesn't mention ventilation or respirator. Doesn't mention cure time.

**R24 C2**: 15

**R24 C3**: What's the most common
thing that goes wrong
and how do you prevent it?

**R24 C4**: REGROUT: Grout cracking (wrong mix ratio), haze left on tiles (not cleaned properly), silicone lifting (wrong type or surface not prepared).
RESURFACE: Peeling/bubbling (surface not prepared or moisture present), orange peel texture (wrong spray distance/pressure), dust in finish (room not cleaned).

Good subs KNOW what goes wrong because they've fixed other people's mistakes.

**R24 C5**: "Nothing goes wrong." This person hasn't done enough work to encounter problems. Or they don't care about quality.

**R25 C2**: 16

**R25 C3**: Do you have your own
tools and vehicle?

**R25 C4**: Yes to both. Lists specific tools. Has a work van or ute with storage for materials. Can show photos of their setup if asked.

**R25 C5**: No vehicle. Borrows tools. Needs you to supply materials. This person is not an independent contractor — engaging them creates sham contracting risk.

**R26 C2**: 17

**R26 C3**: Do you work for other
companies / have other
clients?

**R26 C4**: YES. This is critical. An independent contractor MUST have multiple clients or the capacity to take on other work. If they work exclusively for you → sham contracting risk.

Ideal answer: "Yes, I do work for [other company] and also take direct jobs."

**R26 C5**: "No, I'll just work for you." This is a sham contracting red flag. Under the 2024 Fair Work Act amendments, the "whole of relationship test" looks at whether they genuinely operate their own business.

**R27 C2**: 18

**R27 C3**: How do you handle a
callback / warranty claim?

**R27 C4**: Good: "I go back and fix it at no charge. It's my reputation." Has had callbacks before and resolved them. Understands that callbacks happen even to good tradespeople.

This tells you whether they'll cooperate when (not if) a customer complains.

**R27 C5**: "That never happens to me." Or: "That's the customer's problem." Do not hire this person. They will fight you on every callback and you'll end up paying twice.

**R28 C2**: 19

**R28 C3**: Are you comfortable with
photo documentation?
Before/during/after on
every job?

**R28 C4**: Must say yes. Your quality control depends on photo evidence. If they resist documentation → they're hiding sloppy work.

"Yes, I already do that for my own records" = great sign.

**R28 C5**: "Why do you need photos?" Resistance to documentation = quality problem waiting to happen.

**R29 C2**: 20

**R29 C3**: What days/hours
are you available?
How many jobs per week?

**R29 C4**: Establishes capacity. A regrout sub can typically do 1-2 jobs per day. A resurface sub typically does 1 job per day (longer process).

You need to know their availability to dispatch efficiently.

**R29 C5**: Only available 2 days a week AND has no other clients = either they're not serious or they're not actually a contractor (sham risk).
